<p>The <b>International Standard Book Number</b> (<b>ISBN</b>) is a unique<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[a]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[b]</a></sup> numeric commercial <a href="/wiki/Book" title="Book">book</a> <a href="/wiki/Identifier" title="Identifier">identifier</a>.</p>
<p>An ISBN is assigned to each edition and variation (except reprintings) of a book. For example, an <a href="/wiki/E-book" title="E-book">e-book</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Paperback" title="Paperback">paperback</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Hardcover" title="Hardcover">hardcover</a> edition of the same book would each have a different ISBN. The ISBN is 13 digits long if assigned on or after 1 January 2007, and 10 digits long if assigned before 2007. The method of assigning an ISBN is nation-based and varies from country to country, often depending on how large the publishing industry is within a country.</p>
<p>The initial ISBN configuration of recognition was generated in 1967 based upon the 9-digit <b>Standard Book Numbering</b> (<b>SBN</b>) created in 1966. The 10-digit ISBN format was developed by the <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">International Organization for Standardization</a> (ISO) and was published in 1970 as international standard ISO 2108 (the SBN code can be converted to a ten digit ISBN by prefixing it with a zero).</p>
<p>Occasionally, a book may appear without a printed ISBN if it is printed privately or the author does not follow the usual ISBN procedure; however, this can be rectified later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Another identifier, the <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Serial_Number" title="International Standard Serial Number">International Standard Serial Number</a> (ISSN), identifies periodical publications such as <a href="/wiki/Magazine" title="Magazine">magazines</a>; and the <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Music_Number" title="International Standard Music Number">International Standard Music Number</a> (ISMN) covers for musical scores.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Standard_Book_Number&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Standard Book Numbering (SBN) <a href="/wiki/Code" title="Code">code</a> is a 9-digit commercial <a href="/wiki/Book" title="Book">book</a> <a href="/wiki/Identifier" title="Identifier">identifier</a> system created by <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Foster" title="Gordon Foster">Gordon Foster</a>, Emeritus Professor of Statistics at <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Dublin" title="Trinity College, Dublin">Trinity College, Dublin</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-From_Archive.org-4646971_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-From_Archive.org-4646971-4">[2]</a></sup> for the booksellers and stationers <a href="/wiki/WHSmith" title="WHSmith">WHSmith</a> and others in 1965.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-history-5">[3]</a></sup> The ISBN configuration of recognition was generated in 1967 by David Whitaker (regarded as the "Father of the ISBN"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-niso.org_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-niso.org-6">[4]</a></sup>) and Emery Koltay (who later became director of the <a class="external text" href="http://commerce.bowker.com/standards/home/isbn/us/isbnus.asp" rel="nofollow">U.S. ISBN agency, Bowker</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-niso.org_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-niso.org-6">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 10-digit ISBN format was developed by the <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">International Organization for Standardization</a> (ISO) and was published in 1970 as international standard ISO 2108.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-history-5">[3]</a></sup> The United Kingdom continued to use the 9-digit SBN code until 1974. ISO has appointed the International ISBN Agency as the registration authority for ISBN worldwide and the ISBN Standard is developed under the control of ISO Technical Committee 46/Subcommittee 9 <a href="/wiki/TC_46/SC_9" title="TC 46/SC 9">TC 46/SC 9</a>. The <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">ISO</a> on-line facility only refers back to 1978.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>An SBN may be converted to an ISBN by prefixing the digit "0". For example, the second edition of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mr._J._G._Reeder_Returns" title="Mr. J. G. Reeder Returns">Mr. J. G. Reeder Returns</a></i>, published by Hodder in 1965, has <span class="nowrap">"SBN 340 01381 8"</span> - 340 indicating the publisher, 01381 their serial number, and 8 being the check digit. This can be converted to <a class="internal mw-magiclink-isbn" href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/0340013818">ISBN 0-340-01381-8</a>; the check digit does not need to be re-calculated.</p>
<p>Since 1 January 2007, ISBNs have contained 13 digits, a format that is compatible with "<a href="/wiki/Bookland" title="Bookland">Bookland</a>" European Article Number <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Article_Number" title="European Article Number">EAN</a>-13s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Standard_Book_Number&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An ISBN is assigned to each edition and variation (except reprintings) of a book. For example, an ebook, a paperback, and a hardcover edition of the same book would each have a different ISBN.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[9]</a></sup> The ISBN is 13 digits long if assigned on or after 1 January 2007, and 10 digits long if assigned before 2007. An International Standard Book Number consists of 4 parts (if it is a 10 digit ISBN) or 5 parts (for a 13 digit ISBN):</p>

<ol>
<li>for a 13-digit ISBN, a prefix element – a <i><a href="/wiki/GS1" title="GS1">GS1</a> prefix</i>: so far 978 or 979 have been made available by GS1,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISBN_User_Man_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-ISBN_User_Man-12">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>the <i>registration group element</i>, (language-sharing country group, individual country or territory)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li>the <i>registrant</i> element,</li>
<li>the <i>publication element</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISBN_User_Man_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-ISBN_User_Man-12">[10]</a></sup> and</li>
<li>a <i><a href="/wiki/Checksum" title="Checksum">checksum</a> character</i> or <a href="/wiki/Check_digit" title="Check digit">check digit</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISBN_User_Man_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-ISBN_User_Man-12">[10]</a></sup></li>
</ol>
<p>A 13-digit ISBN can be separated into its parts (<i>prefix element</i>, <i>registration group</i>, <i>registrant</i>, <i>publication</i> and <i>check digit</i>), and when this is done it is customary to separate the parts with <a href="/wiki/Hyphen" title="Hyphen">hyphens</a> or spaces. Separating the parts (<i>registration group</i>, <i>registrant</i>, <i>publication</i> and <i>check digit</i>) of a 10-digit ISBN is also done with either hyphens or spaces. Figuring out how to correctly separate a given ISBN number is complicated, because most of the parts do not use a fixed number of digits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="How_ISBNs_are_issued">How ISBNs are issued</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Standard_Book_Number&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: How ISBNs are issued">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>ISBN issuance is country-specific, in that ISBNs are issued by the ISBN registration agency that is responsible for that country or territory regardless of the publication language. The ranges of ISBNs assigned to any particular country are based on the publishing profile of the country concerned, and so the ranges will vary depending on the number of books and the number, type, and size of publishers that are active. Some ISBN registration agencies are based in national libraries or within ministries of culture and thus may receive direct funding from government to support their services. In other cases, the ISBN registration service is provided by organisations such as bibliographic data providers that are not government funded. In Canada, ISBNs are issued at no cost with the stated purpose of encouraging Canadian culture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[13]</a></sup> In the United Kingdom, United States, and some other countries, where the service is provided by non-government-funded organisations, the issuing of ISBNs requires payment of a fee.</p>
<p><b>Australia:</b> ISBNs are issued by the commercial library services agency Thorpe-Bowker,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[14]</a></sup> and prices range from $42 for a single ISBN (plus a $55 registration fee for new publishers) to $2,890 for a block of 1,000 ISBNs. Access is immediate when requested via their website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Brazil:</b> <a href="/wiki/National_Library_of_Brazil" title="National Library of Brazil">National Library of Brazil</a>, a government agency, is responsible for issuing ISBNs, and there is a cost of R$16 <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISBN_BR_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-ISBN_BR-18">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Canada:</b> <a href="/wiki/Library_and_Archives_Canada" title="Library and Archives Canada">Library and Archives Canada</a>, a government agency, is responsible for issuing ISBNs, and there is no cost. Works in French are issued an ISBN by the <a href="/wiki/Biblioth%C3%A8que_et_Archives_nationales_du_Qu%C3%A9bec" title="Bibliothèque et Archives nationales du Québec">Bibliothèque et Archives nationales du Québec</a>.</p>
<p><b>Colombia:</b> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=C%C3%A1mara_Colombiana_del_Libro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cámara Colombiana del Libro (page does not exist)">Cámara Colombiana del Libro</a>, a NGO, is responsible for issuing ISBNs. Cost of issuing an ISBN is about USD 20.</p>
<p><b>India:</b> The Raja Rammohun Roy National Agency for ISBN(Book Promotion and Copyright Division), under Department of Higher Education, a constituent of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Human_Resource_Development" title="Ministry of Human Resource Development">Ministry of Human Resource Development</a>, is responsible for registration of Indian publishers, authors, universities, institutions, and government departments that are responsible for publishing books.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Italy:</b> The privately held company <i>EDISER srl</i>, owned by <i>Associazione Italiana Editori</i> (Italian Publishers Association) is responsible for issuing ISBNs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[18]</a></sup> The original national prefix 978-88 is reserved for publishing companies, starting at €49 for a ten-codes block<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ediser-tariffs_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-ediser-tariffs-21">[19]</a></sup> while a new prefix 979-12 is dedicated to self-publishing authors, at a fixed price of €25 for a single code.</p>
<p><b>Maldives:</b> The <a href="/wiki/National_Bureau_of_Classification_(NBC)" title="National Bureau of Classification (NBC)">National Bureau of Classification (NBC)</a> is responsible for ISBN registrations for publishers who are publishing in the Maldives.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><b>Malta:</b> The National Book Council (<a href="/wiki/Maltese_language" title="Maltese language">Maltese</a>: Il-Kunsill Nazzjonali tal-Ktieb) issues ISBN in <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>New Zealand:</b> The <a href="/wiki/National_Library_of_New_Zealand" title="National Library of New Zealand">National Library of New Zealand</a> is responsible for ISBN registrations for publishers who are publishing in New Zealand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NLNZ_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-NLNZ-23">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Pakistan:</b> The <a href="/wiki/National_Library_of_Pakistan" title="National Library of Pakistan">National Library of Pakistan</a> is responsible for ISBN registrations for Pakistani publishers, authors, universities, institutions, and government departments that are responsible for publishing books.</p>
<p><b>South Africa:</b> The <a href="/wiki/National_Library_of_South_Africa" title="National Library of South Africa">National Library of South Africa</a> is responsible for ISBN issuance for South African publishing institutions and authors.</p>
<p><b>United Kingdom and Republic of Ireland:</b> The privately held company <i>Nielsen Book Services Ltd</i>, part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nielsen_Holdings" title="Nielsen Holdings">Nielsen Holdings</a> N.V., is responsible for issuing ISBNs in blocks of 10, 100 or 1000. Prices start from £120 (plus <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VAT" title="VAT">VAT</a>) for the smallest block on a standard turnaround of ten days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>United States:</b> In the United States, the privately held company <a href="/wiki/R.R._Bowker" title="R.R. Bowker">R.R. Bowker</a> issues ISBNs. There is a charge that varies depending upon the number of ISBNs purchased, with prices starting at $125.00 for a single number. Access is immediate when requested via their website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Publishers and authors in other countries obtain ISBNs from their respective national ISBN registration agency. A directory of ISBN agencies is available on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_ISBN_Agency" title="International ISBN Agency">International ISBN Agency</a> website.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Registration_group_identifier">Registration group identifier</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Standard_Book_Number&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Registration group identifier">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><span id="Registration_Group_identifier"></span> The <a href="/wiki/List_of_ISBN_identifier_groups" title="List of ISBN identifier groups">registration group identifier</a> is a 1- to 5-digit number that is valid within a single prefix element (i.e. one of 978 or 979).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISBN_User_Man_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-ISBN_User_Man-12">[10]</a></sup> Registration group identifiers have primarily been allocated within the 978 prefix element.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISBN_Ranges_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-ISBN_Ranges-26">[24]</a></sup> The single-digit group identifiers within the 978 prefix element are: 0 or 1 for English-speaking countries; 2 for French-speaking countries; 3 for German-speaking countries; 4 for Japan; 5 for Russian-speaking countries; and 7 for People's Republic of China. An example 5-digit group identifier is 99936, for <a href="/wiki/Bhutan" title="Bhutan">Bhutan</a>. The allocated group IDs are: 0–5, 600–621, 7, 80–94, 950–989, 9926–9989, and 99901–99976.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[25]</a></sup> Books published in rare languages typically have longer group identifiers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Within the 979 prefix element, the registration group identifier 0 is reserved for compatibility with <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Music_Number" title="International Standard Music Number">International Standard Music Numbers</a> (ISMNs), but such material is not actually assigned an ISBN.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISBN_User_Man_12-4"><a href="#cite_note-ISBN_User_Man-12">[10]</a></sup> The registration group identifiers within prefix element 979 that have been assigned are 10 for France, 11 for the Republic of Korea, and 12 for Italy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-C4WDefault-5691881_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-C4WDefault-5691881-29">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>The original 9-digit standard book number (SBN) had no registration group identifier, but prefixing a zero (0) to a 9-digit SBN creates a valid 10-digit ISBN.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Registrant_element">Registrant element</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Standard_Book_Number&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Registrant element">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The national ISBN agency assigns the registrant element (<a href="/wiki/Cf." title="Cf.">cf.</a> <a href="/wiki/Category:ISBN_agencies" title="Category:ISBN agencies">Category:ISBN agencies</a>) and an accompanying series of ISBNs within that registrant element to the publisher; the publisher then allocates one of the ISBNs to each of its books. In most countries, a book publisher is not required by law to assign an ISBN; however, most book stores only handle ISBN bearing merchandise.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>A listing of more than 900,000 assigned publisher codes is published, and can be ordered in book form (<a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>1399, <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>1959). The web site of the ISBN agency does not offer any free method of looking up publisher codes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[28]</a></sup> Partial lists have been compiled (from library catalogs) for the English-language groups: <a href="/wiki/List_of_group-0_ISBN_publisher_codes" title="List of group-0 ISBN publisher codes">identifier 0</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_group-1_ISBN_publisher_codes" title="List of group-1 ISBN publisher codes">identifier 1</a>.</p>
<p>Publishers receive blocks of ISBNs, with larger blocks allotted to publishers expecting to need them; a small publisher may receive ISBNs of one or more digits for the registration group identifier, several digits for the registrant, and a single digit for the publication element. Once that block of ISBNs is used, the publisher may receive another block of ISBNs, with a different registrant element. Consequently, a publisher may have different allotted registrant elements. There also may be more than one registration group identifier used in a country. This might occur once all the registrant elements from a particular registration group have been allocated to publishers.</p>
<p>By using variable block lengths, registration agencies are able to customise the allocations of ISBNs that they make to publishers. For example, a large publisher may be given a block of ISBNs where fewer digits are allocated for the registrant element and many digits are allocated for the publication element; likewise, countries publishing many titles have few allocated digits for the registration group identifier and many for the registrant and publication elements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[29]</a></sup> Here are some sample ISBN-10 codes, illustrating block length variations.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Check_digits">Check digits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Standard_Book_Number&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Check digits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Check_digit" title="Check digit">check digit</a> is a form of redundancy check used for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Error_detection" title="Error detection">error detection</a>, the decimal equivalent of a binary <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Check_bit" title="Check bit">check bit</a>. It consists of a single digit computed from the other digits in the number. The method for the ten digit code is an extension of that for SBNs, the two systems are compatible, and SBN prefixed with "0" will give the same check-digit as without - the digit is base eleven, and can be 0-9 or X. The system for thirteen digit codes is not compatible and will, in general, give a different check digit from the corresponding 10 digit ISBN, and does not provide the same protection against transposition. This is because the thirteen digit code was required to be compatible with the EAN format, and hence could not contain an "X".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="ISBN-10_check_digits">ISBN-10 check digits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Standard_Book_Number&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: ISBN-10 check digits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 2001 edition of the official manual of the <a class="external text" href="http://www.isbn-international.org/" rel="nofollow">International ISBN Agency</a> says that the ISBN-10 <a href="/wiki/Check_digit" title="Check digit">check digit</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[31]</a></sup> – which is the last digit of the ten-digit ISBN – must range from 0 to 10 (the symbol X is used for 10), and must be such that the sum of all the ten digits, each multiplied by its (integer) weight, descending from 10 to 1, is a multiple of <a href="/wiki/11_(number)" title="11 (number)">11</a>.</p>

<p>The two most common errors in handling an ISBN (e.g., typing or writing it) are a single altered digit or the transposition of adjacent digits. It can be <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_proof" title="Mathematical proof">proved</a> that all possible valid ISBN-10's have at least two digits different from each other. It can also be proved that there are no pairs of valid ISBN-10's with eight identical digits and two transposed digits. (These are true only because the ISBN is less than 11 digits long, and because 11 is a <a href="/wiki/Prime_number" title="Prime number">prime number</a>.) The ISBN check digit method therefore ensures that it will always be possible to detect these two most common types of error, i.e. if either of these types of error has occurred, the result will never be a valid ISBN – the sum of the digits multiplied by their weights will never be a multiple of 11. However, if the error occurs in the publishing house and goes undetected, the book will be issued with an invalid ISBN.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>In contrast, it is possible for other types of error, such as two altered non-transposed digits, or three altered digits, to result in a valid ISBN number (although it is still unlikely).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="ISBN-10_check_digit_calculation">ISBN-10 check digit calculation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Standard_Book_Number&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: ISBN-10 check digit calculation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Modular_arithmetic" title="Modular arithmetic">Modular arithmetic</a> is convenient for calculating the check digit using modulus 11. Each of the first nine digits of the ten-digit ISBN—excluding the check digit itself—is multiplied by a number in a sequence from 10 to 2, and the remainder of the sum, with respect to 11, is computed. The resulting remainder, plus the check digit, must equal a multiple of 11 (either 0 or 11). Therefore, the check digit is (11 minus the remainder of the sum of the products modulo 11) modulo 11. Taking the remainder modulo 11 a second time accounts for the possibility that the first remainder is 0. Without the second modulo operation the calculation could end up with 11 – 0 = 11 which is invalid. (Strictly speaking the <i>first</i> "modulo 11" is unneeded, but it may be considered to simplify the calculation.)</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="ISBN-13_check_digit_calculation">ISBN-13 check digit calculation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Standard_Book_Number&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: ISBN-13 check digit calculation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The calculation of an ISBN-13 check digit begins with the first 12 digits of the thirteen-digit ISBN (thus excluding the check digit itself). Each digit, from left to right, is alternately multiplied by 1 or 3, then those products are summed <a href="/wiki/Modular_arithmetic" title="Modular arithmetic">modulo</a> 10 to give a value ranging from 0 to 9. Subtracted from 10, that leaves a result from 1 to 10. A zero (0) replaces a ten (10), so, in all cases, a single check digit results.</p>
<p>For example, the ISBN-13 check digit of 978-0-306-40615-<i>?</i> is calculated as follows:</p>
<pre>
s = 9×1 + 7×3 + 8×1 + 0×3 + 3×1 + 0×3 + 6×1 + 4×3 + 0×1 + 6×3 + 1×1 + 5×3
  =   9 +  21 +   8 +   0 +   3 +   0 +   6 +  12 +   0 +  18 +   1 +  15
  = 93
93 / 10 = 9 remainder 3
10 –  3 = 7
</pre>
<p>Thus, the check digit is 7, and the complete sequence is ISBN 978-0-306-40615-7.</p>

<p>This check system – similar to the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Product_Code" title="Universal Product Code">UPC</a> check digit formula – does not catch all errors of adjacent digit transposition. Specifically, if the difference between two adjacent digits is 5, the check digit will not catch their transposition. For instance, the above example allows this situation with the 6 followed by a 1. The correct order contributes 3×6+1×1 = 19 to the sum; while, if the digits are transposed (1 followed by a 6), the contribution of those two digits will be 3×1+1×6 = 9. However, 19 and 9 are congruent modulo 10, and so produce the same, final result: both ISBNs will have a check digit of 7. The ISBN-10 formula uses the <a href="/wiki/Prime_number" title="Prime number">prime</a> modulus 11 which avoids this blind spot, but requires more than the digits 0-9 to express the check digit.</p>
<p>Additionally, if the sum of the 2nd, 4th, 6th, 8th, 10th, and 12th digits is tripled then added to the remaining digits (1st, 3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, 11th, and 13th), the total will always be divisible by 10 (i.e., end in 0).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="ISBN-10_to_ISBN-13_conversion">ISBN-10 to ISBN-13 conversion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Standard_Book_Number&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: ISBN-10 to ISBN-13 conversion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The conversion is quite simple as one only needs to prefix "978" to the existing number and calculate the new checksum using the ISBN-13 algorithm.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Errors_in_usage"><span id="Cancelled_ISBN"></span>Errors in usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Standard_Book_Number&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Errors in usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Publishers and <a href="/wiki/Library" title="Library">libraries</a> have varied policies about the use of the ISBN check digit. Publishers sometimes fail to check the correspondence of a book title and its ISBN before publishing it; that failure causes book identification problems for libraries, booksellers, and readers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[34]</a></sup> For example, <a class="internal mw-magiclink-isbn" href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/0590764845">ISBN 0-590-76484-5</a> is shared by two books – <i>Ninja gaiden® : a novel based on the best-selling game by Tecmo</i> (1990) and <i>Wacky Laws</i> (1997), both published by <a href="/wiki/Scholastic_Corporation" title="Scholastic Corporation">Scholastic</a>.</p>
<p>Most libraries and booksellers display the book record for an invalid ISBN issued by the publisher. The <a href="/wiki/Library_of_Congress" title="Library of Congress">Library of Congress</a> catalogue contains books published with invalid ISBNs, which it usually tags with the phrase "Cancelled ISBN".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[35]</a></sup> However, book-ordering systems such as <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" title="Amazon.com">Amazon.com</a> will not search for a book if an invalid ISBN is entered to its search engine.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/OCLC" title="OCLC">OCLC</a> often indexes by invalid ISBNs, if the book is indexed in that way by a member library.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="eISBN">eISBN</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Standard_Book_Number&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: eISBN">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Only the term "ISBN" should be used; the terms "eISBN" and "e-ISBN" have historically been sources of confusion and should be avoided. If a book exists in one or more digital (<a href="/wiki/E-book" title="E-book">e-book</a>) formats, each of those formats must have its own ISBN. In other words, each of the three separate <a href="/wiki/EPUB" title="EPUB">EPUB</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Kindle" title="Amazon Kindle">Amazon Kindle</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PDF" title="PDF">PDF</a> formats of a particular book will have its own specific ISBN. They should not share the ISBN of the paper version, and there is no generic "eISBN" which encompasses all the e-book formats for a title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="EAN_format_used_in_barcodes.2C_and_upgrading">EAN format used in barcodes, and upgrading</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Standard_Book_Number&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: EAN format used in barcodes, and upgrading">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Currently the <a href="/wiki/Barcode" title="Barcode">barcodes</a> on a book's back cover (or inside a mass-market <a href="/wiki/Paperback" title="Paperback">paperback</a> book's front cover) are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Article_Number" title="European Article Number">EAN-13</a>; they may have a separate barcode encoding five digits for the <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recommended_retail_price" title="Recommended retail price">recommended retail price</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[37]</a></sup> For 10 digit ISBNs, the number "978", the <a href="/wiki/Bookland" title="Bookland">Bookland</a> "country code", is prefixed to the ISBN in the barcode data, and the check digit is recalculated according to the EAN13 formula (modulo 10, 1x and 3x weighting on alternate digits).</p>
<p>Partly because of an expected shortage in certain ISBN categories, the <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">International Organization for Standardization</a> (ISO) decided to migrate to a thirteen-digit ISBN (ISBN-13). The process began 1 January 2005 and was planned to conclude 1 January 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[38]</a></sup> As of 2011, all the 13-digit ISBNs began with 978. As the 978 ISBN supply is exhausted, the 979 prefix was introduced. Part of the 979 prefix is reserved for use with the <a href="/wiki/Bookland" title="Bookland">Musicland</a> code for <a href="/wiki/Sheet_music" title="Sheet music">musical scores</a> with an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ISMN" title="ISMN">ISMN</a>. 10 digit ISMN codes differed visually as they began with an "M" letter; the bar code represents the "M" as a zero (0), and for checksum purposes it counted as a 3. All ISMNs are now 13 digits commencing 979-0; 979-1 to 979-9 will be used by ISBN.</p>
<p>Publisher identification code numbers are unlikely to be the same in the 978 and 979 ISBNs, likewise, there is no guarantee that language area code numbers will be the same. Moreover, the ten-digit ISBN check digit generally is not the same as the thirteen-digit ISBN check digit. Because the GTIN-13 is part of the <a href="/wiki/Global_Trade_Item_Number" title="Global Trade Item Number">Global Trade Item Number</a> (GTIN) system (that includes the GTIN-14, the GTIN-12, and the GTIN-8), the 13-digit ISBN falls within the 14-digit data field range.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>Barcode format compatibility is maintained, because (aside from the group breaks) the ISBN-13 barcode format is identical to the EAN barcode format of existing 10-digit ISBNs. So, migration to an EAN-based system allows booksellers the use of a single numbering system for both books and non-book products that is compatible with existing ISBN based data, with only minimal changes to <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">information technology</a> systems. Hence, many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bookstore" title="Bookstore">booksellers</a> (e.g., <a href="/wiki/Barnes_%26_Noble" title="Barnes &amp; Noble">Barnes &amp; Noble</a>) migrated to EAN barcodes as early as March 2005. Although many American and Canadian booksellers were able to read EAN-13 barcodes before 2005, most general retailers could not read them. The upgrading of the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Product_Code" title="Universal Product Code">UPC</a> <a href="/wiki/Barcode_system" title="Barcode system">barcode system</a> to full EAN-13, in 2005, eased migration to the ISBN-13 in North America.</p>

<li id="cite_note-13"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-13">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Some books have several codes in the first block: e.g. A. M. Yaglom's <i>Correlation Theory...</i>, published by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Springer_Verlag" title="Springer Verlag">Springer Verlag</a>, has two ISBNs, 0-387-96331-6 and 3-540-96331-6. Though Springer's 387 and 540 codes are different for English (0) and German (3); the same item number 96331 produces the same check digit: 6. Springer uses 431 as their publisher code for Japanese (4) and 4-431-96331-? would also have check digit ? = 6. Other Springer books in English have publisher code 817, and 0-817-96331-? would also get check digit ? = 6. This suggests special considerations were made for assigning Springer's publisher codes, as random assignments of different publisher codes would <i>not</i> lead the same item number to get the same check digit every time. Finding publisher codes for English and German, say, with this effect amounts to solving a linear equation in modular arithmetic.</span></li>
<li id="cite_note-14"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-14">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">The international ISBN agency's <i>ISBN User's Manual</i> says: "The ten-digit number is divided into four parts of variable length, which must be separated clearly, by hyphens or spaces" although omission of separators is permitted for internal data processing. If present, hyphens must be correctly placed; see <a class="external text" href="https://www.isbn-international.org/range_file_generation" rel="nofollow">ISBN hyphenation definition</a>. The actual definition for hyphenation contains more than 220 different registration group elements with each one broken down into a few to several ranges for the length of the registrant element (more than 1,000 total). The document defining the ranges, listed by agency, is 29 pages.</span></li>
