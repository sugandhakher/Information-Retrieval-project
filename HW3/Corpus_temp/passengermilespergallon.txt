<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><b>Passenger miles per gallon</b> (PMPG) is a <a href="/wiki/Metric_(mathematics)" title="Metric (mathematics)">metric</a> to evaluate the <a href="/wiki/Energy_conversion_efficiency" title="Energy conversion efficiency">energy efficiency</a> of a vehicle or transportation mode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The PMPG can be obtained by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Multiply" title="Multiply">multiplying</a> the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miles_per_gallon" title="Miles per gallon">miles per gallon</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fuel" title="Fuel">fuel</a> (MPG) by either the <a href="/wiki/Seating_capacity" title="Seating capacity">passenger capacity</a> or the average occupancy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The occupancy of personal vehicles is typically lower than capacity by a considerable degree <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and thus the values computed based on capacity and on occupancy will often be quite different.</p>
