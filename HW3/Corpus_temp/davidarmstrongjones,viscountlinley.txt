<p><b>David Albert Charles Armstrong-Jones, Viscount Linley</b> (born 3 November 1961), known professionally as <b>David Linley</b>, is an English furniture maker and chairman of the auction house <a href="/wiki/Christie%27s" title="Christie's">Christie's</a> UK. The son of <a href="/wiki/Princess_Margaret,_Countess_of_Snowdon" title="Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon">Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Antony_Armstrong-Jones,_1st_Earl_of_Snowdon" title="Antony Armstrong-Jones, 1st Earl of Snowdon">Antony Armstrong-Jones, 1st Earl of Snowdon</a>, he is a grandson of King <a href="/wiki/George_VI" title="George VI">George VI</a>, is 18th in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Line_of_succession_to_the_British_throne" title="Line of succession to the British throne">line to succeed</a> <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a>, his maternal aunt, and is the heir-apparent to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earldom_of_Snowdon" title="Earldom of Snowdon">Earldom of Snowdon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RoyalSuccession_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-RoyalSuccession-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Armstrong-Jones,_Viscount_Linley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the age of five, Linley started lessons in the Buckingham Palace schoolroom with his cousin, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Andrew,_Duke_of_York" title="Prince Andrew, Duke of York">Prince Andrew</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-M.R._2-0"><a href="#cite_note-M.R.-2">[2]</a></sup> He went to several <a href="/wiki/Independent_school" title="Independent school">independent schools</a>: first, to Gibbs Pre-Preparatory School in <a href="/wiki/Kensington" title="Kensington">Kensington</a> in London,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> followed by the pre-preparatory section of <a href="/wiki/Ashdown_House,_East_Sussex" title="Ashdown House, East Sussex">Ashdown House School</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Sussex" title="East Sussex">East Sussex</a>, then on to <a class="external text" href="http://www.education.gov.uk/edubase/establishment/summary.xhtml?urn=123305" rel="nofollow">Millbrook House School</a>, near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abingdon,_Oxfordshire" title="Abingdon, Oxfordshire">Abingdon</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire" title="Oxfordshire">Oxfordshire</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and finally to <a href="/wiki/Bedales_School" title="Bedales School">Bedales School</a>, where he developed a passion for arts and crafts. From 1980 to 1982, he studied at Parnham College in the small town of <a href="/wiki/Beaminster" title="Beaminster">Beaminster</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>, for craftsmen in wood.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_life">Professional life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Armstrong-Jones,_Viscount_Linley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Linley,_Burlington_Arcade,_London_01.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3456" data-file-width="4608" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Linley%2C_Burlington_Arcade%2C_London_01.jpg/220px-Linley%2C_Burlington_Arcade%2C_London_01.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Linley%2C_Burlington_Arcade%2C_London_01.jpg/330px-Linley%2C_Burlington_Arcade%2C_London_01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Linley%2C_Burlington_Arcade%2C_London_01.jpg/440px-Linley%2C_Burlington_Arcade%2C_London_01.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Linley,_Burlington_Arcade,_London_01.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Linley store, <a href="/wiki/Burlington_Arcade" title="Burlington Arcade">Burlington Arcade</a>, London</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Linley opened a workshop in <a href="/wiki/Dorking" title="Dorking">Dorking</a> where he designed and made furniture for three years before setting up his own company David Linley Furniture Ltd, now known as LINLEY, where he makes <a href="/wiki/Bespoke" title="Bespoke">bespoke</a> furniture, upholstery, and interior design products known for their neoclassical appearance and use of inlaid woods. He has written numerous books and lectured around the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Christ_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Christ-5">[5]</a></sup> His work is sold in retail stores in <a href="/wiki/Belgravia" title="Belgravia">Belgravia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harrods" title="Harrods">Harrods</a> and overseas including the <a href="/wiki/Bespoke_Collection" title="Bespoke Collection">Bespoke Collection</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schneider_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Schneider-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 December 2006, Linley took up the post of chairman of <a href="/wiki/Christie%27s" title="Christie's">Christie's</a> UK, having joined the board in 2005 as a non-executive director.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Christ_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Christ-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life_and_family">Personal life and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Armstrong-Jones,_Viscount_Linley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 8 April 2002, Linley, along with <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">the Prince of Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Andrew,_Duke_of_York" title="Prince Andrew, Duke of York">the Duke of York</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward,_Earl_of_Wessex" title="Prince Edward, Earl of Wessex">the Earl of Wessex</a>, stood guard at the lying-in-state of their grandmother, <a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_The_Queen_Mother" title="Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother">Queen Elizabeth, The Queen Mother</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> This was a mark of respect unofficially known as the <a href="/wiki/Vigil_of_the_Princes" title="Vigil of the Princes">Vigil of the Princes</a>, which had taken place only once before, during the lying-in-state of <a href="/wiki/George_V" title="George V">King George V</a> in 1936.</p>
<p>Linley had romances with <a href="/wiki/Susannah_Constantine" title="Susannah Constantine">Susannah Constantine</a>, Kate Menzies and Nicola Formby. On 8 October 1993, he married <a href="/wiki/Serena_Armstrong-Jones,_Viscountess_Linley" title="Serena Armstrong-Jones, Viscountess Linley">the Hon. Serena Stanhope</a>, a daughter of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Stanhope,_12th_Earl_of_Harrington" title="Charles Stanhope, 12th Earl of Harrington">Viscount Petersham (later the 12th Earl of Harrington)</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Margaret%27s,_Westminster" title="St. Margaret's, Westminster">St. Margaret's Church, Westminster</a>.</p>
<p>Viscount Linley and his wife have two children:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>The Hon.</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_Armstrong-Jones" title="Charles Armstrong-Jones"><b>Charles</b> Patrick Inigo Armstrong-Jones</a> (born 1 July 1999, now aged 17)</li>
<li><i>The Hon.</i> <a href="/wiki/Margarita_Armstrong-Jones" title="Margarita Armstrong-Jones"><b>Margarita</b> Elizabeth Rose Alleyne Armstrong-Jones</a> (born 14 May 2002, now aged 14)</li>
</ul>
<p>Linley has one full sister, <a href="/wiki/Lady_Sarah_Chatto" title="Lady Sarah Chatto">Lady Sarah Chatto</a> (<i>née</i> Armstrong-Jones); and one paternal half-sister, Lady Frances von Hofmannsthal (née Armstrong-Jones). Viscount Linley also has a half-brother, Jasper Cable-Alexander, son of his father and Melanie Cable-Alexander, an editor at <a href="/wiki/Country_Life_(magazine)" title="Country Life (magazine)"><i>Country Life</i></a> magazine.</p>
<p>From 2000–2002, Linley, his wife and son lived in <a href="/wiki/Kensington_Palace" title="Kensington Palace">Kensington Palace</a> with his mother, Princess Margaret, in her declining years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Linley's daughter, <a href="/wiki/Margarita_Armstrong-Jones" title="Margarita Armstrong-Jones">Margarita Armstrong-Jones</a>, was a bridesmaid at the wedding of the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Cambridge" title="Duke of Cambridge">Duke of Cambridge</a>.</p>
<p>The Linleys have three homes: a flat in <a href="/wiki/Chelsea,_London" title="Chelsea, London">Chelsea, London</a>; a cottage on the <a href="/wiki/Daylesford,_Gloucestershire" title="Daylesford, Gloucestershire">Daylesford estate</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire" title="Gloucestershire">Gloucestershire</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chateau_d%27Autet" title="Chateau d'Autet">Chateau d'Autet</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/Luberon" title="Luberon">Luberon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Provence" title="Provence">Provence</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Titles.2C_styles_and_arms">Titles, styles and arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Armstrong-Jones,_Viscount_Linley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Titles, styles and arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Title">Title</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Armstrong-Jones,_Viscount_Linley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Title">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Though his mother was a princess, in the United Kingdom royal titles and styles are passed only from the father. He does not hold the title <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Snowdon" title="Earl of Snowdon">Viscount Linley</a> in his own right; rather, it is a <a href="/wiki/Courtesy_title" title="Courtesy title">courtesy title</a> used by the eldest son and <a href="/wiki/Heir_apparent" title="Heir apparent">heir apparent</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Snowdon" title="Earl of Snowdon">Earl of Snowdon</a>.</p>
<p><br/></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancestry">Ancestry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Armstrong-Jones,_Viscount_Linley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Ancestry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Linley is the son of <a href="/wiki/Princess_Margaret,_Countess_of_Snowdon" title="Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon">Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Antony_Armstrong-Jones,_1st_Earl_of_Snowdon" title="Antony Armstrong-Jones, 1st Earl of Snowdon">1st Earl of Snowdon</a>; thus he is the nephew of <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a>, and a grandson of <a href="/wiki/George_VI" title="George VI">George VI</a>. At the time of his birth he was fifth in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Line_of_succession_to_the_British_throne" title="Line of succession to the British throne">line of succession to the British throne</a>; he is currently in eighteenth place and is the first person in the line of succession who is not a descendant of the Queen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RoyalSuccession_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-RoyalSuccession-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
