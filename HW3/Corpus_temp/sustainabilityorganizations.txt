<p><b>Sustainability <a href="/wiki/Organization" title="Organization">organizations</a></b> seek to implement <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a> strategies which provide them with economic and cultural benefits attained through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_responsibility" title="Environmental responsibility">environmental responsibility</a>. Recently,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (August 2015)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> the natural environment has become a key strategic issue in both the <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">business</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academic_community" title="Academic community">academic communities</a>. Through "implementing sustainability strategies, firms can integrate long-run profitability with their efforts to protect the <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystem</a>, providing them with opportunities to achieve the traditional competitive advantages of &amp; cost leadership and market differentiation via environmental responsibility".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Sustainability strategies have been persistently employed in a number<sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="This term requires quantification. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition">Definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A sustainable system generally can be defined in environmental terminology as "a living system which operates in a way that it does not use up resources more quickly than they can be naturally replenished; a sustainable economic system operates in a way so that expenditures are either equal or less than the income." Sustainable social systems maintain that all members are allowed to contribute, thereby synthesizing the final product. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_sustainability">Corporate sustainability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Corporate sustainability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corporate_Sustainability" title="Corporate Sustainability">Corporate Sustainability</a> refers to "a company’s activities, voluntary by definition, demonstrating the inclusion of social and environmental concerns in business operations and in interactions with stakeholders".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Each individual organization should choose its own particular goals and approaches as they pertain to corporate sustainability, matching the organization’s aims and intentions and aligning with the organization’s strategy, as an appropriate response to the conditions in which it functions.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture">Agriculture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Agriculture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sustainable agriculture incorporates design and management procedures that coincide with natural processes in order to conserve all resources and minimize waste production and damage to natural systems, while preserving or improving farm profitability. Sustainable agriculture systems are "designed to take the greatest advantage of current soil nutrient and water cycles, energy flows, beneficial soil organisms, and natural pest controls. These systems also seek to produce food that is nutritious yet uncontaminated with products such as synthetically compound fertilizers, pesticides, growth regulators, and livestock feed additives. Sustainable agriculture systems often depend on crop rotations, crop residues, animal manures, legumes, green manures, off-farm organic wastes, appropriate mechanical cultivation or minimal tillage to optimize soil biological and natural pest control activity in order to maintain soil fertility and crop productivity."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Also, resistant species varieties, and biological and cultural controls are implemented to control pests, weeds and diseases. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Sustainable agriculture organizations aim to educate and encourage farmers to implement sustainable agriculture practices into their productions. Many sponsor research and education outreach projects.</p>

<li><b><a href="/wiki/Sightline_Institute" title="Sightline Institute">Sightline Institute</a></b> formerly Northwest Environment Watch, a non-profit organization that aims to foster a sustainable economy and way of life in the Pacific Northwest, a bioregion defined by the watersheds of rivers flowing through North America's temperate rainforest zone</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Building">Building</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Building">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"By using more efficient building methods and materials, it is estimated that we could reduce the energy, resource consumption and / or waste production by 50-60% without decreasing value, aesthetics or function."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Taking the Earth's finite resources into consideration, along with acknowledging that manufactured products, including all building materials, affect the Earth’s resources, it is becoming very important to make sensible decisions regarding the use of these limited resources to preserve our natural environment along with human civilization. Increased use of resource efficient construction materials and methods will help to induce more sustainable practices throughout the building and construction industry. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<ul>
<li><b>Building Green</b> authoritative information on environmentally responsible building design and construction from the publishers of Environmental Building News<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>oikos</b> detailed information on sustainable design and construction<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></li>

<li><b><a href="/wiki/U.S._Green_Building_Council" title="U.S. Green Building Council">U.S. Green Building Council</a></b> the council is the nation's foremost coalition of leaders from across the building industry working to promote buildings that are environmentally responsible, profitable, and healthy places to live and work</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Business">Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sustainable business organizations participate in environmentally friendly or green practices in order to make certain that all processes, products, and manufacturing activities sufficiently address current environmental concerns while still retaining a profit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Concurrently, it is a business that “meets the needs of the present world without compromising the ability of the future generations to meet their own needs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> It is the method of evaluating how to design products that will correspond to current environmental conditions and how well a company’s products perform with renewable resources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> There are many organizations and networks currently interacting with businesses in order to integrate sustainability into their central goals and contribute to the environmentally and socially responsible business movement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Sustainable business organizations:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><b>Business and Industry Resource Venture</b>, provides free information, assistance and referrals to help Seattle businesses improve their environmental performance<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Business for Social Responsibility</b> a business membership organization<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Center for a Sustainable Economy</b> non-partisan research and policy organization that promotes innovative tax and other market-based approaches to achieving a sustainable economy<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>CERES</b> the largest coalition of environmental, investor, and advocacy groups working together for sustainable prosperity. These groups form a community of forward-looking companies that have committed to continuous environmental improvement by endorsing the CERES principles, a ten-point code of environmental conduct.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Cool Companies</b> A guide for businesses seeking to cut energy costs and reduce pollution.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Future 500</b> brings together strategically selected companies, NGOs, and opinion leaders to overcome mutual distrust and advance systemic solutions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Global Environmental Management Initiative</b> the Global Environmental Management Initiative standard reporting for environmental, economic and social activity<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>GreenBiz</b> A resource center on business, the environment, and the bottom line<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Lifestyles of Health and Sustainability (LOHAS)</b> a market segment focused on health and fitness, the environment, personal development, sustainable living, and social justice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Net_Impact" title="Net Impact">Net Impact</a></b> a network of emerging business leaders committed to using the power of business to create a better world. It is also the most progressive and influential network of MBAs in existence today. <li><b>Sacramento Area Sustainable Business Program</b> Regional partnership that encourages businesses to adopt environmentally friendly and sustainable practices. Certifies and promotes businesses that take voluntary actions to conserve energy and water, reduce waste and pollution, recycle and purchase recycled products, improve air quality, and implement green building measures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>SWFL Sustainability</b> is a Florida-based initiative focused on people, place, and profit. Based out of Florida, the organization organizes an annual summit that brings leaders from various innovation sectors together to discuss regional and statewide initiatives to improve quality of life for today and tomorrow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Communities">Communities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Communities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<li><b>Sustainable Communities Network</b> for everyone interested in making smart choices about communities—choices that are both economically and ecologically intelligent<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Sustainable Connections</b> working with business and community leaders working to transform and model an economy built on sustainable practices<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Seattle" title="Sustainable Seattle">Sustainable Seattle</a></b> an award winning, volunteer-based civic network and forum located in Seattle with a focus on the metropolitan city/county area</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Global_Ecovillage_Network" title="Global Ecovillage Network">Global Ecovillage Network</a></b> 1500+ pages on ecovillages and sustainability</li>
<li><b>Sustainable Architecture Buildings and Culture</b> ideas for sustainable communities<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Despite the fact that some places may be similar in economic terms, the characteristics of a sustainable business model vary from community to community.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Many organizations aim to assist communities in developing economic opportunities for all citizens. Some programs exist that "assist individuals in obtaining employment, that create jobs and that help individuals become economically self-sufficient." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Economic sustainability organizations:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><b>Center for the Advancement of the Steady State Economy (CASSE)</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> is an organization dedicated to advocating a sustainable economy with stabilized population and consumption; such an economy is called a "steady state economy."</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Environment.2Fenergy">Environment/energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Environment/energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Environmental sustainability is the "long-term maintenance of ecosystem components and functions for future generations." In addition, environmental sustainability is the process of making sure current processes of interaction with the environment are pursued with the idea of keeping the environment as unspoiled as naturally possible. It maintains that the Earth’s resources must not be depleted faster than they can naturally be replenished. Depletion of our finite resources inevitably tries the ability of human civilizations to persist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Environmental sustainability organizations:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Bioneers" title="Bioneers">Bioneers</a></b> working to preserve biological and cultural diversity</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Center_for_a_New_American_Dream" title="Center for a New American Dream">Center for a New American Dream</a></b> New American Dream helps Americans consume responsibly to protect the environment, enhance quality of life, and promote social justice.</li>
<li><b>Climate Solutions</b> promotes a regional approach to global warming solutions<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Friends_of_the_Earth" title="Friends of the Earth">Friends of the Earth</a> (FOE)</b> is an international environmental organization dedicated to preserving the health and diversity of the planet for future generations</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/International_Institute_for_Environment_and_Development" title="International Institute for Environment and Development">International Institute for Environment and Development</a> (IIED)</b> promotes sustainable patterns of world development through collaborative research, policy studies, networking and knowledge dissemination</li>
<li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Union_for_the_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for the Conservation of Nature">International Union for the Conservation of Nature</a> (IUCN)</b> seeks to conserve the integrity and diversity of nature and to ensure that any use of natural resources is equitable and ecologically sustainable</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Northwest_Earth_Institute" title="Northwest Earth Institute">Northwest Earth Institute</a></b> a pioneer in taking earth-centered education programs to people where they spend their time-in their neighborhoods, workplaces, homes, schools, and centers of faith</li>
<li><b>Renewable Energy Policy Project (REPP)</b> supports the advancement of renewable energy technology through policy research <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountain_Institute" title="Rocky Mountain Institute">Rocky Mountain Institute</a></b> We work extensively with the private sector, as well as with civil society and government, to create abundance by design and to apply the framework of natural capitalism.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Sightline_Institute" title="Sightline Institute">Sightline Institute</a></b> Sightline Institute is an independent, nonprofit research and communications center — a think tank — founded by Alan Durning in 1993.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Union_of_Concerned_Scientists" title="Union of Concerned Scientists">Union of Concerned Scientists</a></b> UCS combines independent scientific research and citizen action to develop innovative, practical solutions and to secure responsible changes in government policy, corporate practices, and consumer choices.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Zero_Emissions_Research_and_Initiatives" title="Zero Emissions Research and Initiatives">Zero Emissions Research and Initiatives</a> (ZERI)</b> a global network of creative minds seeking solutions to world challenges. The common vision shared by the members of the ZERI family is to view waste as resource and seek solutions using nature's design principles as inspiration.</li>
<li><b>Zero Waste Alliance</b> The Zero Waste Alliance is a national leader providing assistance to industry sectors and organizations for development and implementation of standards, tools and practices that lead to a more sustainable future through the reduction and elimination of waste and toxics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Law_and_policy">Law and policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Law and policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"Regulations, incentives and policies affecting sustainable agriculture are rooted in environmental legislation and laws on the international, Federal, state and local level." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Law and policy sustainability organizations:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Earth_Policy_Institute" title="Earth Policy Institute">Earth Policy Institute</a></b> dedicated to building an environmentally sustainable economy; raising awareness to support public response to population growth, rising CO2, loss of species, and other trends that are affecting the Earth</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Global_Exchange" title="Global Exchange">Global Exchange</a></b> nonprofit organization that envisions a people-centered globalization that values the rights of workers and the health of the planet; that prioritizes international collaboration as central to ensuring peace; and that aims to create a local, green economy designed to embrace the diversity of our communities.</li>
<li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apollo_Alliance" title="Apollo Alliance">Apollo Alliance</a></b> a coalition of labor, business, environmental, and community leaders working to catalyze a clean energy revolution that will put millions of Americans to work in a new generation of high-quality, green-collar jobs. Inspired by the Apollo space program, this organization promotes investments in energy efficiency, clean power, mass transit, next-generation vehicles, and emerging technology, as well as in education and training.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Institute_for_Local_Self-Reliance" title="Institute for Local Self-Reliance">Institute for Local Self-Reliance</a></b> A comprehensive resource for policymakers, organizations and activists looking for innovative public policies enacted around the world that can be used to make communities vibrant and strong!” The site, run by The Institute for Local Self-Reliance (ILSR), proposes a “new set of rules” which supports humanly scaled politics and economics.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="News">News</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: News">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>News sustainability organizations:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><b>EcoSnoop</b> objective is to make people aware of ways to become greener<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>World Changing</b> is a nonprofit media organization headquartered in Seattle, WA, that comprises a global network of independent journalists, designers and thinkers. We cover the world’s most innovative solutions to the planet’s problems, and inspire readers around the world with stories of new tools, models and ideas for building a bright green future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Worldwatch_Institute" title="Worldwatch Institute">Worldwatch Institute</a></b> dedicated to fostering the evolution of an environmentally sustainable society—one in which human needs are met in ways that do not threaten the health of the natural environment or the prospects of future generations</li>
<li><b>Triple Pundit</b> is an online publication dedicated to promoting the concepts of sustainability to a business audience. The name is a reference to the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Triple_Bottom_Line" title="Triple Bottom Line">Triple Bottom Line</a>" which seeks to balance economy, society, and the environment - a key tenet to sustainable thinking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-profit">Non-profit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Non-profit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nonprofit organizations "play a part in society through their social value creation." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> Many nonprofit organizations have adopted an organizational sustainability focus in both strategic and operational levels of management. Present nonprofit organizations have been required to adopt strategies aimed at establishing viable, sustainable organizations so that they can maintain to pursue their social mission. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Non-profit sustainability organizations:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Action_Without_Borders" title="Action Without Borders">Action Without Borders</a></b> a nonprofit organization founded in 1995 with offices in the United States and Argentina. It runs Idealist.org, an interactive site where people and organizations can exchange resources and ideas, locate opportunities and supporters, and take steps toward building a world where all people can lead free and dignified lives.</li>
<li><b>Cielito Lindo Ranch</b> a non-profit organization operating off-the-grid in southwest New Mexico focusing on research into lost-cost easy to implement sustainable technologies that anyone can use to simplify and improve their life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Corporate_Watch" title="Corporate Watch">Corporate Watch</a></b> A non-profit organization committed to “holding corporations accountable.” Functions as a resource on corporate globalization research, news stories, and ways to take action. Also, organizes campaigns against political campaign financing, greenwashing, and war-profiteering.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Earth_Island_Institute" title="Earth Island Institute">Earth Island Institute</a></b> works to find solutions to environmental problems by developing and supporting projects that promote the conservation, preservation and restoration of the Earth</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Environmental_Defense_Fund" title="Environmental Defense Fund">Environmental Defense Fund</a></b> Environmental Defense Fund is a leading national nonprofit organization representing more than 700,000 members. Guided by science, Environmental Defense Fund evaluates environmental problems and works to create and advocate solutions that win lasting political, economic and social support.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Environmental_Working_Group" title="Environmental Working Group">Environmental Working Group</a> (EWG)</b> a not-for-profit environmental research organization that uses the power of information to improve public health and protect the environment by reducing pollution in air, water and food. Based in Washington, D.C., and with an office in Oakland, California, EWG conducts groundbreaking, computer-assisted research on a variety of environmental issues.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Green_Cross_International" title="Green Cross International">Green Cross International</a></b> non-profit NGO founded by Mikhail Gorbachev with a mission to help create a sustainable future by cultivating harmonious relationships between humans and the environment</li>
<li><b>Shajar Dost Foundation</b> (Pakistan) Shajar Dost is working to increase forest cover in Pakistan. Pakistan has the highest deforesttion rate in Asia. Shajar Dost is planting suplings in Murree at Mussyari on three hundred acres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Social_Venture_Network" title="Social Venture Network">Social Venture Network</a> (SVN)</b> promotes new models and leadership for socially and environmentally sustainable business in the 21st century.” As a nonprofit network, they do this by championing member initiatives, information services, and community forums.</li>
<li><b>Sustainability International</b> a non-profit organization dedicated to providing innovative solutions to complex sustainability and conservation issues in the developing world. Sustainability International's current large-scale initiative centers around revitalizing the Niger Delta, an area devastated by five decades of oil pollution.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Fair_Trade_USA" title="Fair Trade USA">Fair Trade USA</a></b> nonprofit organization, is one of twenty members of Fairtrade Labelling Organizations International (FLO), and the only third-party certifier of Fair Trade products in the United States.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Population_concern">Population concern</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Population concern">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Population_Matters#Population_concern" title="Population Matters">Population concern</a> organizations aim to reduce the <a href="/wiki/Ecological_footprint" title="Ecological footprint">ecological footprint</a> of the human species by encouraging and promoting ethical policies and practices that will result in lower levels of population.</p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_organizations_promoting_population_moderation" title="List of organizations promoting population moderation">List of organizations promoting population moderation</a></div>
<p>Notable population concern organizations include <a href="/wiki/Population_Connection" title="Population Connection">Population Connection</a> and <a href="/wiki/Population_Matters" title="Population Matters">Population Matters</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Schools">Schools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Schools">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Organizations that offer global education resources, products, and services. Many educational organizations offer their own global education programs in the area of environmental sustainability, or resources for the development of sustainability programs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Specialist sustainability schools:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Antioch_University_Seattle" title="Antioch University Seattle">Antioch University Seattle</a></b> has master's and PhD level programs approaching environmental policy and sustainability issues from both a social science and natural science perspective.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Pinchot_University" title="Pinchot University">Pinchot University</a></b> offers both an MBA in Sustainable Business and a Certificate in Sustainable Business. In these programs, students work with distinguished faculty from top business schools to master proven sustainability practices.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/The_Evergreen_State_College" title="The Evergreen State College">The Evergreen State College</a></b> offers a highly interdisciplinary model that sets the stage for successful curricular work in sustainability. Learning communities use whole systems thinking to approach the social and environmental aspects of sustainability. Evergreen offers programs in sustainability and a Masters in Environmental Studies.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/IslandWood" title="IslandWood">IslandWood</a></b> on Bainbridge Island has an educational mission to inspire environmental and community stewardship.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Networking">Networking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Networking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Many organizations provide an outlet for expanding and cultivating social networks in order to attain the most beneficial responses for various goals among sustainability stakeholders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> In other words, "many sustainability networking organizations are a catalyst in bringing about systemic change by giving decision makers a shared, science-based understanding of sustainability and a unifying framework for developing a sustainable society."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Sustainability networking organizations:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a></b> uses non-violent direct action and creative communication to expose global environmental problems and to promote solutions that are essential to a green and peaceful future</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Sierra_Club" title="Sierra Club">Sierra Club</a></b> is one of the US' oldest, largest environmental organizations for the protection, restoration, and responsible use of earth’s resources; site has extensive resources for information, networking, and action.</li>
</ul>
