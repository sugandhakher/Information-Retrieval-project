<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="hatnote" role="note">This article is about the generic term. For the intergovernmental organization, see <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>. <p><b>Commonwealth</b> is a traditional English term for a political community founded for the <a href="/wiki/Common_good" title="Common good">common good</a>. Historically it has sometimes been synonymous with "<a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>".</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> noun "commonwealth" in the sense meaning "public welfare; general good or advantage" dates from the 15th century. The original phrase "the common-wealth" or "the common weal" (echoed in the modern synonym "public weal") comes from the old meaning of "wealth", which is "well-being", and is itself a loose translation of the Latin <a href="/wiki/Res_publica" title="Res publica">res publica</a> (republic). The term literally meant "common well-being". In the 17th century the definition of "commonwealth" expanded from its original sense of "public welfare" or "commonweal" to mean "a state in which the supreme power is vested in the people; a republic or democratic state". "Better things were done, and better managed ... under a Commonwealth than under a King." Pepys, <i>Diary</i> (1667)</p>
<p>Three countries – <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dominica" title="Dominica">Dominica</a> – have the official title "Commonwealth", as do four <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. states</a> and two <a href="/wiki/Territories_of_the_United_States" title="Territories of the United States">U.S. territories</a>. More recently, the term has been used for fraternal associations of some sovereign nations, most notably the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>, an association primarily of former members of the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a>, which is often referred to as simply "the Commonwealth".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_use">Historical use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Historical use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rome">Rome</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rome">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Translations of Roman writers' works to English have on occasion translated "<i><a href="/wiki/Res_publica" title="Res publica">Res publica</a></i>", and variants thereof, to "the commonwealth", a term referring to the Roman state as a whole.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="England">England</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: England">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_England" title="Commonwealth of England">Commonwealth of England</a> was the official name of the political unit (<i>de facto</i> military rule in the name of parliamentary supremacy) that replaced the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">Kingdom of England</a> (after the <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">English Civil War</a>) from 1649–53 and 1659–60, under the rule of <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Cromwell" title="Oliver Cromwell">Oliver Cromwell</a> and his son and successor <a href="/wiki/Richard_Cromwell" title="Richard Cromwell">Richard</a>. From 1653 to 1659, although still legally known as a Commonwealth, the republic, united with the former <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Scotland" title="Kingdom of Scotland">Kingdom of Scotland</a>, operated under different institutions (at times as a <i>de facto</i> monarchy) and is known by historians as the Protectorate. In a British context, it is sometimes referred to as the "Old Commonwealth".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Iceland">Iceland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Iceland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_Commonwealth" title="Icelandic Commonwealth">Icelandic Commonwealth</a> or the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Icelandic_Free_State" title="Icelandic Free State">Icelandic Free State</a> (<a href="/wiki/Icelandic_language" title="Icelandic language">Icelandic</a>: Þjóðveldið) was the state existing in Iceland between the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Althing" title="Althing">Althing</a> in 930 and the pledge of fealty to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_Norway" title="King of Norway">Norwegian king</a> in 1262. It was initially established by a public consisting largely of recent immigrants from <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> who had fled the unification of that country under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King" title="King">King</a> <a href="/wiki/Harald_Fairhair" title="Harald Fairhair">Harald Fairhair</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Poland.E2.80.93Lithuania">Poland–Lithuania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Poland–Lithuania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Lithuanian_Commonwealth" title="Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth">Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth</a></div>
<p><i>Republic</i> is still an alternative translation of the traditional name of the <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Lithuanian_Commonwealth" title="Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth">Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth</a>. <a href="/wiki/Wincenty_Kad%C5%82ubek" title="Wincenty Kadłubek">Wincenty Kadłubek</a> (Vincent Kadlubo, 1160–1223) used for the first time the original Latin term <i><a href="/wiki/Res_publica" title="Res publica">res publica</a></i> in the context of Poland in his "Chronicles of the Kings and Princes of Poland". The name was used officially for the confederal country formed by <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a> 1569–1795.</p>
<p>It is also often referred as "<a href="/wiki/Golden_Liberty" title="Golden Liberty">Nobles' Commonwealth</a>" (1505–1795, i.e., before the union). In contemporary political doctrine of Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth, "our state is a Republic (or Commonwealth) under presidency of the King". The Commonwealth introduced a doctrine of religious tolerance called <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_Confederation" title="Warsaw Confederation">Warsaw Confederation</a>, had its own parliament <i><a href="/wiki/Sejm" title="Sejm">Sejm</a></i> (although elections were restricted to <a href="/wiki/Nobility" title="Nobility">nobility</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elected_king" title="Elected king">elected kings</a>, who were bound to certain contracts <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacta_conventa_(Poland)" title="Pacta conventa (Poland)">Pacta conventa</a></i> from the beginning of the reign).</p>
<p>"A commonwealth of good counsaile" was the title of the 1607 English translation of the work of <a href="/wiki/Wawrzyniec_Grzyma%C5%82a_Go%C5%9Blicki" title="Wawrzyniec Grzymała Goślicki">Wawrzyniec Grzymała Goślicki</a> "De optimo senatore" that presented to English readers many of the ideas present in the political system of the Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_use">Current use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Current use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_level">International level</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International level">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>"Commonwealth" was first proposed as a term for a federation of the six Australian <a href="/wiki/Crown_colony" title="Crown colony">crown colonies</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_Convention_(Australia)#1891_convention" title="Constitutional Convention (Australia)">1891 constitutional convention</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>. Its adoption was initially controversial, as it was associated by some with the republicanism of <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Cromwell" title="Oliver Cromwell">Oliver Cromwell</a> (<a href="#England">see above</a>), but it was retained in all subsequent drafts of the constitution.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The term was finally incorporated into law in the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Australia_Constitution_Act" title="Commonwealth of Australia Constitution Act">Commonwealth of Australia Constitution Act</a> 1901</i>, which established the federation. Australia operates under a federal system, in which power is divided between the federal (national) government and the <a href="/wiki/States_and_territories_of_Australia" title="States and territories of Australia">state governments</a> (the successors of the six colonies). So, in an Australian context, the term "Commonwealth" (capitalised) refers to the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Australia" title="Government of Australia">federal government</a>, and "Commonwealth of Australia" is the official name of the country.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Bahamas">The Bahamas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: The Bahamas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">The Bahamas</a> uses the official style <i>Commonwealth of The Bahamas</i>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Dominica">Dominica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Dominica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The small <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> republic of <a href="/wiki/Dominica" title="Dominica">Dominica</a> has used the official style <i>Commonwealth of Dominica</i> since 1970.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Federated_states_and_territories">Federated states and territories</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Federated states and territories">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The term "commonwealth" has one of two political meanings within the United States:</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="U.S._states">U.S. states</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: U.S. states">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_(U.S._state)" title="Commonwealth (U.S. state)">Commonwealth (U.S. state)</a></div>
<p>Four <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">states</a> in the United States officially designate themselves as "commonwealths". All four were original colonies or parts thereof (Kentucky was originally a part of the land grant of the <a href="/wiki/Colony_of_Virginia" title="Colony of Virginia">Colony of Virginia</a>) and share a strong influence of colonial common law in some of their laws and institutions. The four are:</p>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Kentucky" title="Kentucky">Kentucky</a></b> is designated a Commonwealth by the <a href="/wiki/Kentucky_Constitution" title="Kentucky Constitution">Kentucky Constitution</a> and is known constitutionally as the "Commonwealth of Kentucky".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a></b> is a Commonwealth,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> declaring itself as such in its constitution, which states: "[T]he body politic is formed by a voluntary association of individuals: it is a social compact, by which the whole people covenants with each citizen, and each citizen with the whole people, that all shall be governed by certain laws for the common good."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a></b> uses the "Commonwealth of Pennsylvania" constitutionally and in its official title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a></b> has been known as the "Commonwealth of Virginia" since before joining the United States, and is referred to as a Commonwealth in its constitution.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="U.S._insular_areas">U.S. insular areas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: U.S. insular areas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_(U.S._insular_area)" title="Commonwealth (U.S. insular area)">Commonwealth (U.S. insular area)</a></div>
<p>"Commonwealth" is also used in the United States to describe the political relationship between the United States and the overseas <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Incorporated_territory" title="Incorporated territory">unincorporated territories</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_the_Philippines" title="Commonwealth of the Philippines">Commonwealth of the Philippines</a></b> – formed in 1934, and became independent in 1946, upon which "Commonwealth" was replaced by "Republic".</li>
<li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Commonwealth of Puerto Rico">Commonwealth of Puerto Rico</a></b> – became a commonwealth in 1952.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_the_Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands">Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands</a></b> – became a commonwealth in 1978.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_bodies">International bodies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: International bodies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Commonwealth_of_Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Commonwealth of Nations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>—formerly the British Commonwealth—is a voluntary association of 53 independent sovereign states, most of which were once part of the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a>. The Commonwealth's membership includes both republics and monarchies. The head of the Commonwealth of Nations is Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Elizabeth II</a>, who reigns as monarch directly in 16 member states known as <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_realm" title="Commonwealth realm">Commonwealth realms</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Commonwealth_of_Independent_States">Commonwealth of Independent States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Commonwealth of Independent States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States" title="Commonwealth of Independent States">Commonwealth of Independent States</a> (CIS) is a loose <a href="/wiki/Military_alliance" title="Military alliance">alliance</a> or confederation consisting of 10 of the 15 <a href="/wiki/Post-Soviet_states" title="Post-Soviet states">former Soviet Republics</a>, the exceptions being <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a> (a CIS associate member), <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>. <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> left the CIS in August 2008 after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_South_Ossetia_war" title="2008 South Ossetia war">a clash</a> with <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> over <a href="/wiki/South_Ossetia" title="South Ossetia">South Ossetia</a>. Its creation signalled the dissolution of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, its purpose being to "allow a civilised divorce" between the <a href="/wiki/Republics_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Republics of the Soviet Union">Soviet Republics</a>. The CIS has developed as a forum by which the member-states can co-operate in <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">economics</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defence_(military)" title="Defence (military)">defence</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Foreign_policy" title="Foreign policy">foreign policy</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_use">Proposed use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Proposed use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commonwealth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Labour MP <a href="/wiki/Tony_Benn" title="Tony Benn">Tony Benn</a> sponsored a <i><a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Britain_Bill" title="Commonwealth of Britain Bill">Commonwealth of Britain Bill</a></i> several times between 1991 and 2001, intended to abolish the monarchy and establish a British republic. It never reached <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_reading" title="Second reading">second reading</a>.</p>
