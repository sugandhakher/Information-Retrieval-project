<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><b>Renewable energy in Bangladesh</b> refers to the use of <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a> to <a href="/wiki/Electricity_generation" title="Electricity generation">generate electricity</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>. The current renewable energy comes from <a href="/wiki/Biogas" title="Biogas">biogas</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydro_power" title="Hydro power">hydro power</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solar_power" title="Solar power">solar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">wind</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solar_power">Solar power</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Bangladesh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Solar power">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The long term average sunshine data indicates that the period of bright sunshine hours in the coastal regions of Bangladesh varies from 3 to 11 hours daily.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harijan_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Harijan-3">[3]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Insolation" title="Insolation">insolation</a> in Bangladesh varies from 3.8 kwh/m<sup>2</sup>/day to 6.4 kwh/m<sup>2</sup>/day at an average of 5 kwh/m<sup>2</sup>/day. These indicate that there are good prospects for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_thermal" title="Solar thermal">solar thermal</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photovoltaic" title="Photovoltaic">photovoltaic</a> application in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>With an estimated 40% of the population in Bangladesh having no access to electricity, the government introduced a scheme known as solar home systems (SHS) to provide electricity to households with no grid access.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The program reached 3 million households as of late 2014 and, with more than 50,000 systems being added per month since 2009, the World Bank has called it "the fastest growing solar home system program in the world."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Bangladeshi government is working towards universal electricity access by 2021 with the SHS program projected to cover 6 million households by 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wind_power">Wind power</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Bangladesh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Wind power">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The long term wind flow, especially in the islands and the southern coastal belt of Bangladesh indicate that the average <a href="/wiki/Wind_speed" title="Wind speed">wind speed</a> remains between 3 and 4.5 m/s for the months of March to September and 1.7 to 2.3 for remaining period of the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> There is a good opportunity in island and coastal areas for the application of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wind_mill" title="Wind mill">wind mills</a> for pumping and electrification. But during the summer and monsoon seasons (March to October) there can be very low pressure areas and storm wind speeds 200 to 300 kmph can be expected. <a href="/wiki/Wind_turbine" title="Wind turbine">Wind turbines</a> have to be strong enough to withstand these high wind speeds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tidal_power">Tidal power</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Bangladesh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tidal power">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The tides at <a href="/wiki/Chittagong_Division" title="Chittagong Division">Chittagong Division</a> are predominantly semidiurnal with a large variation in range corresponding to the seasons, the maximum occurring during the south-west monsoon. In 1984, an attempt was made by mechanical engineering department of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KUET" title="KUET">KUET</a> to assess the feasibility of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tidal_energy" title="Tidal energy">tidal energy</a> in the coastal regions of Bangladesh, especially at <a href="/wiki/Cox%27s_Bazar" title="Cox's Bazar">Cox's Bazar</a> and at the islands of <a href="/wiki/Maheshkhali_Island" title="Maheshkhali Island">Maheshkhali</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kutubdia" title="Kutubdia">Kutubdia</a>. The average <a href="/wiki/Tidal_range" title="Tidal range">tidal range</a> was found within 4-5 meter and the <a href="/wiki/Amplitude" title="Amplitude">amplitude</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spring_tide" title="Spring tide">spring tide</a> exceeds even 6 meter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> From different calculations, it is anticipated that there are a number of suitable sites at Cox's Bazar, Maheshkhali, Kutubdia and other places where permanent basins with pumping arrangements might be constructed which would be a double operation scheme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thedailystar_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-thedailystar-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wave_energy">Wave energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Bangladesh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Wave energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bangladesh has favorable conditions for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wave_energy" title="Wave energy">wave energy</a> especially during the period beginning from late March to early October. Waves generated in <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">Bay of Bengal</a> and a result of the southwestern wind is significant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thedailystar2_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-thedailystar2-13">[13]</a></sup> Maximum <a href="/wiki/Wave_height" title="Wave height">wave height</a> of over 2 meter with an absolute maximum of 2.4 meter were recorded. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wave_period" title="Wave period">wave periods</a> varied from 3 to 4 seconds for waves of about 0.5 meter and about 6 seconds for waves of about 2 meter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harijan_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Harijan-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Waste_to_electric_energy">Waste to electric energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Bangladesh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Waste to electric energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In order to save the large cities from environment pollution, the <a href="/wiki/Waste_management" title="Waste management">waste management</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Waste-to-energy" title="Waste-to-energy">electricity generation</a> from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solid_waste" title="Solid waste">solid wastes</a> programme is being taken by the government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biogas">Biogas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Bangladesh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Biogas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There mainly two types of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biogas_plant" title="Biogas plant">biogas plants</a> used in Bangladesh, floating dome type and fixed dome type. Bag type plants are also used in the country but rarely.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bssnews_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-bssnews-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
