<p><b>Mark Diesendorf</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> academic and environmentalist, known for his work in sustainable development and renewable energy. He currently teaches environmental studies at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_New_South_Wales" title="University of New South Wales">University of New South Wales</a> (UNSW), Australia. He was formerly professor of environmental science and founding director of the Institute for Sustainable Futures at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Technology,_Sydney" title="University of Technology, Sydney">University of Technology, Sydney</a> and before that a principal research scientist with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CSIRO" title="CSIRO">CSIRO</a>, where he was involved in early research on integrating <a href="/wiki/Wind_power_in_Australia" title="Wind power in Australia">wind power</a> into electricity grids. His most recent book is <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainable_Energy_Solutions_for_Climate_Change&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sustainable Energy Solutions for Climate Change (page does not exist)">Sustainable Energy Solutions for Climate Change</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Diesendorf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Diesendorf is the son of the engineer <a href="/wiki/Walter_Diesendorf" title="Walter Diesendorf">Walter Diesendorf</a> and the poet <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Diesendorf" title="Margaret Diesendorf">Margaret Diesendorf</a>. His PhD research was focused on applied mathematics and theoretical physics applied to the solar interior. His early postdoctoral research was diverse, including the analysis of ground and satellite data on VLF emissions, mechanisms of insect smell and vision, and biological catalysts. From 1975 to 1985 he worked in the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Scientific_and_Industrial_Research_Organisation" title="Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation">CSIRO</a> Division of Mathematics, the Australian national research organisation, on topics such as the integration of wind power into electricity grids.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Silence_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Silence-1">[1]</a></sup> He became a Principal Research Scientist and leader of the Applied Mathematics group in CSIRO.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He left CSIRO in 1985 after the organisation had terminated all research on renewable energy. From 1996 to 2001 he was Professor of Environmental Science and Founding Director of the Institute for Sustainable Futures at UTS and then director of a company Sustainability Centre Pty Ltd.</p>
<p>From 2004 to 2016, Diesendorf has been a Senior Lecturer and then Associate Professor and Deputy Director of the <a class="external text" href="http://www.ies.unsw.edu.au" rel="nofollow">Institute of Environmental Studies</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_NSW" title="University of NSW">University of NSW</a>, now called UNSW Australia. In 2015 IES was abolished by the university and Diesendorf retired (nominally) in mid-2016 to become Honorary Associate Professor at UNSW. He continues to teach (as a guest lecturer), research and consult in the interdisciplinary fields of <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_energy" title="Sustainable energy">sustainable energy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_transport" title="Sustainable transport">sustainable urban transport</a>, theory of <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecological_economics" title="Ecological economics">ecological economics</a>, and practical processes by which government, business and other organisations can achieve ecologically sustainable and socially just development.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ies_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ies-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Based on his belief that science, technology and economics should serve the community at large, Dr Diesendorf has been at various times the Secretary of the Society for Social Responsibility in Science (Australian Capital Territory), President of the Australia New Zealand Society for Ecological Economics, co-founder and Vice-President of the Sustainable Energy Industries Council of Australia, and co-founder and President of the original Australasian Wind Energy Association.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ies_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-ies-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Much of his recent and current research is on climate mitigation, especially transitioning electricity supply systems to 100% renewable energy. To this end he is involved with colleagues in the Centre for Energy &amp; Environmental Markets at UNSW in scenario development, computer simulation modelling and energy policy. Previously Dr Diesendorf was one of the leading proponents calling for the ethical, scientific debate over public <a href="/wiki/Water_fluoridation" title="Water fluoridation">water fluoridation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On this issue Diesendorf has authored several papers, including a major 1986 article entitled "The mystery of declining tooth decay" in the journal <i>Nature,</i> examining the need for a scientific re-evaluation of fluoridation, and has highlighted some of the contrary evidence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Silence_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Silence-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
