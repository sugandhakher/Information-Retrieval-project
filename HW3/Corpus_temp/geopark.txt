<p>A <b>Geopark</b> is a unified area that advances the protection and use of geological heritage in a sustainable way, and promotes the economic well-being of the people who live there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> There are Global Geoparks and National Geoparks.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concepts">Concepts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geopark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Concepts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <i>Global Geopark</i> is a unified area with geological heritage of international significance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globalgeopark_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-globalgeopark-2">[2]</a></sup> Geoparks use that heritage to promote awareness of key issues facing society in the context of the dynamic planet we all live on. Many geoparks promote awareness of geological <a href="/wiki/Hazard" title="Hazard">hazards</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcanoes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquakes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunamis</a> and many help prepare <a href="/wiki/Disaster" title="Disaster">disaster</a> mitigation strategies among <a href="/wiki/Local_community" title="Local community">local communities</a>. Geoparks hold records of past climate change and are educators on current <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a> as well as adopting a best practise approach to utilising renewable energy and employing the best standards of "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_tourism" title="Green tourism">green tourism</a>".<a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">Tourism</a> industry promotion in geopark, as a geographically sustainable and applicable tourism model, aims to sustain, or even enhance, the geographical character of a place.</p>
<p>Geoparks also inform about the sustainable use and need for <a href="/wiki/Natural_resource" title="Natural resource">natural resources</a>, whether they are mined, quarried or harnessed from the surrounding <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">environment</a> while at the same time promoting respect for the environment and the integrity of the <a href="/wiki/Landscape" title="Landscape">landscape</a>. Geoparks are not a legislative designation though the key heritage sites within a geopark are often protected under local, regional or national legislation as appropriate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globalgeopark_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-globalgeopark-2">[2]</a></sup> The multidisciplinary nature of the concept of geopark and tourism promotion in geoparks differentiates itself from other models of <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_tourism" title="Sustainable tourism">sustainable tourism</a>. In fact, sustainable tourism promotion within geopark actually encompasses many of the subdivisions of sustainable tourism including: geo-tourism (geo-site tourism: as a basic factor), community-based tourism and integrated rural tourism (as a vital needs), <a href="/wiki/Ecotourism" title="Ecotourism">ecotourism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cultural_heritage" title="Cultural heritage">cultural heritage</a> tourism, etc.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_Network_and_UNESCO">Global Network and UNESCO</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geopark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Global Network and UNESCO">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Global_Geoparks_Network" title="Global Geoparks Network">Global Geoparks Network</a> (GGN) is supported by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Educational,_Scientific_and_Cultural_Organization" title="United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization">United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization</a> (UNESCO). Many national geoparks and other local geoparks projects also exist which are not included in the Global Geoparks Network.</p>
<p>The geoparks initiative was launched by UNESCO in response to the perceived need for an international initiative that recognizes sites representing an <a href="/wiki/Earth_science" title="Earth science">earth science</a> interest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UNESCO_Geopark_About_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-UNESCO_Geopark_About-3">[3]</a></sup> Global Geoparks Network aims at enhancing the value of such sites while at the same time creating <a href="/wiki/Employment" title="Employment">employment</a> and promoting regional <a href="/wiki/Economic_development" title="Economic development">economic development</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UNESCO_Geopark_About_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-UNESCO_Geopark_About-3">[3]</a></sup> The Global Geoparks Network works in synergy with UNESCO's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Heritage" title="World Heritage">World Heritage</a> Centre and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Man_and_the_Biosphere" title="Man and the Biosphere">Man and the Biosphere</a> (MAB) World Network of Biosphere Reserves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UNESCO_Geopark_About_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-UNESCO_Geopark_About-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualification">Qualification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geopark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Global Geoparks Network (GGN) is a UNESCO activity established in 1998. According to UNESCO, for a geopark to apply to be included in the GGN, it needs to:</p>
<ul>
<li>have a management plan designed to foster socio-economic development that is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sustainable" title="Sustainable">sustainable</a> based on <a href="/wiki/Geotourism" title="Geotourism">geotourism</a></li>
<li>demonstrate methods for conserving and enhancing geological heritage and provide means for teaching geoscientific disciplines and broader environmental issues</li>
<li>have joint proposals submitted by public authorities, local communities and private interests acting together, which demonstrate the best practices with respect to Earth heritage conservation and its integration into <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a> strategies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
