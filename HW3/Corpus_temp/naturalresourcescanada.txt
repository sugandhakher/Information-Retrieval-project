<p>The <b>Department of Natural Resources</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French:</a> <i>Ministère des Ressources naturelles</i>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Operating_as" title="Operating as">operating under</a> the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Identity_Program" title="Federal Identity Program">FIP applied title</a> <b>Natural Resources Canada</b> (<b>NRCan</b>), is the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_(government_department)" title="Ministry (government department)">ministry</a> of the government of <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> responsible for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natural_resources" title="Natural resources">natural resources</a>, <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mineral" title="Mineral">minerals</a> and <a href="/wiki/Metal" title="Metal">metals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forests</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earth_science" title="Earth science">earth sciences</a>, <a href="/wiki/Map" title="Map">mapping</a> and <a href="/wiki/Remote_sensing" title="Remote sensing">remote sensing</a>. It was created in 1995 by amalgamating the now-defunct Departments of <b>Energy, Mines and Resources</b> and <b>Forestry</b>. Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) works to ensure the responsible development of Canada's natural resources, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Energy_in_Canada" title="Energy in Canada">energy</a>, forests, minerals and metals. NRCan also uses its expertise in earth sciences to build and maintain an up-to-date knowledge base of our landmass and resources. To promote internal collaboration, NRCan has implemented a departmental wide wiki based on <a href="/wiki/MediaWiki" title="MediaWiki">MediaWiki</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Natural Resources Canada also collaborates with <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexican</a> government scientists, along with the <a href="/wiki/Commission_for_Environmental_Cooperation" title="Commission for Environmental Cooperation">Commission for Environmental Cooperation</a>, to produce the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Environmental_Atlas" title="North American Environmental Atlas">North American Environmental Atlas</a>, which is used to depict and track environmental issues for a continental perspective.</p>
<p>Under the Canadian constitution, responsibility for natural resources belongs to the provinces, not the federal government. However, the federal government has jurisdiction over off-shore resources, trade and commerce in natural resources, statistics, international relations, and boundaries. The current <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_Natural_Resources_(Canada)" title="Minister of Natural Resources (Canada)">Minister of Natural Resources</a> is <a href="/wiki/Jim_Carr" title="Jim Carr">Jim Carr</a> as of November 4, 2015.</p>
<p>The department is governed by the Resources and Technical Surveys Act, R.S.C., c.R-7 and the Department of Natural Resources Act, S.C. 1994, c. 41.</p>
<p>"structured along business lines according to types of natural resources and areas of interest." The department currently has these sectors:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Canadian_Forest_Service" title="Canadian Forest Service">Canadian Forest Service</a></li>
<li>Corporate Management and Services Sector</li>
<li>Earth Sciences Sector</li>
<li>Energy Sector</li>
<li>Innovation and Energy Technology Sector</li>
<li>Minerals and Metals Sector</li>
<li>Science and Policy Integration</li>
<li>Public Affairs and Portfolio Management Sector</li>
<li>Shared Services Office</li>
<li>Geographical Names Board of Canada</li>
</ul>
