<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><b>Energy saving lamps</b> are sources of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artificial_light" title="Artificial light">artificial light</a> that employ advanced <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a> to reduce the amount of <a href="/wiki/Electricity" title="Electricity">electricity</a> used to generate light, relative to traditional filament-burning <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_bulb" title="Light bulb">light bulbs</a>.</p>
<p>Examples of energy saving lamps include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fluorescent_lamps" title="Fluorescent lamps">Fluorescent lamps</a>; i.e. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fluorescent_lamp_formats" title="Fluorescent lamp formats">regular</a> and <a href="/wiki/Compact_fluorescent_lamp" title="Compact fluorescent lamp">compact</a></li>
<li>a <a href="/wiki/Light-emitting_diode" title="Light-emitting diode">Light-emitting diode</a> bulb</li>
<li>a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light-emitting_Electrochemical_Cell" title="Light-emitting Electrochemical Cell">Light-emitting Electrochemical Cell</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Electrodeless_lamp#Magnetic_induction_lamps" title="Electrodeless lamp">Magnetic induction lamps</a></li>
</ul>
