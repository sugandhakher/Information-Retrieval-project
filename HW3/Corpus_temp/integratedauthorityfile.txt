<p>The <b>Integrated Authority File</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Gemeinsame Normdatei</i></span>, also known as: <i>Universal Authority File</i>) or <b>GND</b> is an international <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Authority_file" title="Authority file">authority file</a> for the organisation of personal names, subject headings and corporate bodies from <a href="/wiki/Library_catalog" title="Library catalog">catalogues</a>. It is used mainly for <a href="/wiki/Documentation" title="Documentation">documentation</a> in <a href="/wiki/Library" title="Library">libraries</a> and increasingly also by <a href="/wiki/Archive" title="Archive">archives</a> and <a href="/wiki/Museum" title="Museum">museums</a>. The GND is managed by the <a href="/wiki/German_National_Library" title="German National Library">German National Library</a> in cooperation with various regional library networks in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German-speaking_Europe" title="German-speaking Europe">German-speaking Europe</a> and other partners. The GND falls under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CC0" title="CC0">Creative Commons Zero</a> (CC0) license.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The GND specification provides a hierarchy of high-level entities and sub-classes, useful in <a href="/wiki/Library_classification" title="Library classification">library classification</a>, and an approach to unambiguous identification of single elements. It also comprises an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ontology_(computer_science)" title="Ontology (computer science)">ontology</a> intended for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knowledge_representation" title="Knowledge representation">knowledge representation</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Semantic_web" title="Semantic web">semantic web</a>, available in the <a href="/wiki/Resource_Description_Framework" title="Resource Description Framework">RDF</a> format.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Integrated Authority File became operational in April 2012 and integrates the content of the following authority files which have since been discontinued:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Name_Authority_File" title="Name Authority File">Name Authority File</a> (German: <i>Personennamendatei</i>; PND)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Corporate_Bodies_Authority_File" title="Corporate Bodies Authority File">Corporate Bodies Authority File</a> (German: <i>Gemeinsame Körperschaftsdatei</i>; GKD)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Subject_Headings_Authority_File" title="Subject Headings Authority File">Subject Headings Authority File</a> (German: <i>Schlagwortnormdatei</i>; SWD)</li>
<li>Uniform Title File of the Deutsches Musikarchiv (German: <i>Einheitssachtitel-Datei des Deutschen Musikarchivs</i>; DMA-EST)</li>
</ul>
<p>At the time of its introduction ("GND-Grundbestand" from 5 April 2012), the GND holds 9,493,860 files, including 2,650,000 personalized names.</p>
