<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p>The <b>Rio Declaration on Environment and Development</b>, often shortened to <b>Rio Declaration</b>, was a short document produced at the 1992 <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> "Conference on Environment and Development" (UNCED), informally known as the <a href="/wiki/Earth_Summit" title="Earth Summit">Earth Summit</a>. The Rio Declaration consisted of 27 principles intended to guide countries in future <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>. It was signed by over 170 countries.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rio_Declaration_on_Environment_and_Development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The international community met twice to assess the progress made in implementing the principles of the document; first in New York in 1997 during a General Assembly Session of the UN, and then in <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a> in 2002. While the document helped to raise environmental awareness, evidence shows that little has been achieved in the document's environmental goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Robbins2007_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Robbins2007-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content">Content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rio_Declaration_on_Environment_and_Development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Rio Declaration proclaims 27 principles. It includes formulations of the <a href="/wiki/Precautionary_principle" title="Precautionary principle">precautionary principle</a> (principle 15) and of the <a href="/wiki/Polluter_pays_principle" title="Polluter pays principle">polluter pays principle</a> (principle 16).</p>
