<p><b>Renewable energy in Albania</b> ranges from <a href="/wiki/Biomass" title="Biomass">biomass</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geothermal" title="Geothermal">geothermal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hydropower" title="Hydropower">hydropower</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solar_power" title="Solar power">solar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">wind</a> energy in <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ub_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ub-1">[1]</a></sup> Albania relies mostly on hydroelectric resources, therefore, it has difficulties when water levels are low. Albania has a great potential for solar, wind, and geothermal energy. The climate in Albania is Mediterranean so it possesses considerable potential for solar energy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-green_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-green-2">[2]</a></sup> Similarly, the mountain elevations provide good areas for wind projects. There is also potential for geothermal energy because Albania has natural wells.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hydro_power">Hydro power</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Albania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Hydro power">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The current electricity source in Albania is mostly from hydropower plants, however, this is not very reliable since water levels fluctuate. Verbund, an Austria company, and Albania made an agreement to construct the hydropower plant Ashta in 2012. It is estimated to supply power to approximately 100,000 households.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Renewable_Energies_Albania_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Renewable_Energies_Albania-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solar_power">Solar power</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Albania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Solar power">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Solar_power_in_Albania" title="Solar power in Albania">Solar power in Albania</a></div>
<p>The United Nations Development Program is supporting a program to install solar panels in Albania. The program has used $2.75 million to support the installation of 75,000m<sup>2</sup> of solar panels. By 2010, 10,700m<sup>2</sup> of solar panels were installed and by 2014 the target had been met. There are 50,000m<sup>2</sup> of solar panels expected to be installed by 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Renewable_Energies_Albania_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Renewable_Energies_Albania-3">[3]</a></sup> Albania gets about 2100-2700 hours of sunshine in a year so it has a great potential for solar energy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wiki_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-wiki-4">[4]</a></sup>Solar energy is easily accessible since most energy comes directly or indirectly from the sun. It could be used for heating and lighting homes, commercial, and industrial buildings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blog_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-blog-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wind_power">Wind power</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Albania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Wind power">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Albania has potential for wind energy but technologies have not been developed. However, there are plans to develop wind projects in the next years. There is a plan proposing to have 2000MW of wind energy. Wind generates mechanical power through wind turbines. Some geographic locations are better than others because the wind power depends on the speed of the wind. The coastal lowlands and Southern, Eastern, and Northern Albania mountains are good areas for wind turbines. However, there are constraints that take part in choosing location including: altitude, site accessibility, infrastructures, protected areas, and power grid. The wind speed is 8-9m/s in many areas of Albania. Albania might export excess wind energy to Italy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Renewable_Energies_Albania_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Renewable_Energies_Albania-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geothermal_energy">Geothermal energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Albania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geothermal energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Geothermal energy could also be used in Albania. It comes from warm water sources from underground soil. Geothermal energy comes from the heat generated by the Earth. There are some spots called hot spots that generate more heat than others. There are natural wells near Albaniaâ€™s border with Greece. This energy could be used for heating purposes. Geothermal energy in Albania is under study and there have been no attempts to use it yet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-green_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-green-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Laws_and_petitions">Laws and petitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Albania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Laws and petitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Power Sector Law No.9073, approved in 2004, gives permits to construct new hydropower plants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Renewable_Energies_Albania_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Renewable_Energies_Albania-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Concession Law No.9663, approved in 2006, attracts private investments in hydropower plants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Renewable_Energies_Albania_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-Renewable_Energies_Albania-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Albania placed a tariff for existing and new hydropower plants in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Renewable_Energies_Albania_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-Renewable_Energies_Albania-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Electricity Market Model was approved in 2008. It facilitates purchases between independent power producers and small power producers. It allows producers to sell electricity to all markets at agreed terms. Non-household customers can become eligible consumers and choose their energy suppliers. This helps renewable energy to be more accessible.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Renewable_Energies_Albania_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-Renewable_Energies_Albania-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
