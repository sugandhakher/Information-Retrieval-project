<p>A <b>building energy rating</b> (BER) is similar to the <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Energy_label" title="Energy label">energy label</a> for a household <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrical_appliance" title="Electrical appliance">electrical appliance</a>. The label has a scale of A-G, with A-rated buildings the most <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">energy efficient</a> and G the least. In relation to dwellings, the Sustainable Energy Authority of Ireland states that "<i>A BER is an indication of the energy performance of a home. It covers energy use for space heating, water heating, ventilation and lighting calculated on the basis of standard occupancy.</i>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> A BER assessment and certificate may be compulsory to sell a building or shortly after its construction. Methods of calculations and legislations related to BER may be different from one country to another. BER was created as a tool to help monitoring and improving the overall building energy efficiency.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Building_Energy_Rating_in_Ireland">Building Energy Rating in Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Building_energy_rating&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Building Energy Rating in Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a>, each new dwelling built from January 1, 2007 required a BER, unless <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Planning_application" title="Planning application">planning application</a> was lodged prior to December 31, 2006. From January 1, 2009, it became illegal to offer a dwelling for sale without BER.</p>
<p>From July 1, 2008 a BER is necessary for new non-residential buildings where <a href="/wiki/Planning_permission" title="Planning permission">planning permission</a> is required. From January 1, 2009 a BER is required for existing non-residential buildings, when offered for sale or to let.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Statutory_Instrument" title="Statutory Instrument">Statutory Instrument</a> regulating the requirements for BER is "<i>S.I. 666 of 2006</i>". It is Ireland's implementation of the <a href="/wiki/Directive_on_the_energy_performance_of_buildings" title="Directive on the energy performance of buildings">Directive on the energy performance of buildings</a></p>
