<p>An <b>energy crisis</b> is any significant (bottleneck; logistics; or price rise) in the supply of <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a> resources to an <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a>. In popular literature, it often refers to one of the energy sources used at a certain time and place, in particular those that supply <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_electricity_grid" title="National electricity grid">national electricity grids</a> or those used as fuel in vehicles.</p>
<p>Industrial development and population growth have led to a surge in the global <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Energy_demand" title="Energy demand">demand for energy</a> in recent years. In the 00s, this new demand — together with Middle East tension, the falling value of the U.S. dollar, dwindling oil reserves, concerns over <a href="/wiki/Peak_oil" title="Peak oil">peak oil</a>, and oil price speculation — triggered the <a href="/wiki/2000s_energy_crisis" title="2000s energy crisis">2000s energy crisis</a>, which saw the price of oil reach an all-time high of $147.30 a barrel in 2008.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Causes">Causes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_crisis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Causes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Door_paard_getrokken_taxi.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="320" data-file-width="443" height="159" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Door_paard_getrokken_taxi.jpg/220px-Door_paard_getrokken_taxi.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Door_paard_getrokken_taxi.jpg/330px-Door_paard_getrokken_taxi.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Door_paard_getrokken_taxi.jpg/440px-Door_paard_getrokken_taxi.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Government actions like tax hikes, nationalisation of energy companies, and regulation of the energy sector, shift supply and demand of energy away from its economic equilibrium. <a href="/wiki/Market_failure" title="Market failure">Market failure</a> is possible when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_monopoly" title="Government monopoly">monopoly</a> manipulation of markets occurs. A crisis can develop due to industrial actions like <a href="/wiki/Trade_union" title="Trade union">union</a> organized <a href="/wiki/Strike_action" title="Strike action">strikes</a> and government embargoes. The cause may be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Over-consumption" title="Over-consumption">over-consumption</a>, aging <a href="/wiki/Infrastructure" title="Infrastructure">infrastructure</a>, <a href="/wiki/Choke_point" title="Choke point">choke point</a> disruption or bottlenecks at <a href="/wiki/Oil_refinery" title="Oil refinery">oil refineries</a> and port facilities that restrict fuel supply. An emergency may emerge during very cold winters due to increased consumption of energy.</p>
<p>Large fluctuations and manipulations in future <a href="/wiki/Energy_derivative" title="Energy derivative">derivatives</a> can have a substantial impact on price. Large investment banks control 80% of oil derivatives as of May 2012, compared to 30% only a decade ago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> This increase contributed to an improvement of global energy output from 117 687 TWh in 2000 to 143 851TWh in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Limitations on free trade for derivatives could reverse this trend of growth in energy production. Kuwaiti Oil Minister Hani Hussein stated that "Under the supply and demand theory, oil prices today are not justified," in an interview with Upstream.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pipeline failures and other accidents may cause minor interruptions to energy supplies. A crisis could possibly emerge after infrastructure damage from <a href="/wiki/Severe_weather" title="Severe weather">severe weather</a>. Attacks by terrorists or <a href="/wiki/Militia" title="Militia">militia</a> on important infrastructure are a possible problem for energy consumers, with a successful strike on a <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> facility potentially causing global shortages. Political events, for example, when governments change due to regime change, monarchy collapse, <a href="/wiki/Military_occupation" title="Military occupation">military occupation</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup</a> may disrupt oil and gas production and create shortages. Fuel shortage can also be due to the excess and useless use of the fuels.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Emerging_oil_shortage">Emerging oil shortage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_crisis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Emerging oil shortage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Oil_depletion" title="Oil depletion">Oil depletion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peak_oil" title="Peak oil">Peak oil</a></div>
<p>“Peak oil” is the period when the maximum rate of global <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a> <a href="/wiki/Extraction_of_petroleum" title="Extraction of petroleum">extraction</a> is reached, after which the rate of production enters terminal decline. It relates to a long-term decline in the available supply of petroleum. This, combined with increasing demand, significantly increases the worldwide prices of petroleum derived products. Most significant is the availability and price of liquid fuel for transportation.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The US Department of Energy in the <a href="/wiki/Hirsch_report" title="Hirsch report">Hirsch report</a> indicates that “The problems associated with world oil production peaking will not be temporary, and past 'energy crisis' experience will provide relatively little guidance.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mitigation_efforts">Mitigation efforts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_crisis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mitigation efforts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mitigation_of_peak_oil" title="Mitigation of peak oil">Mitigation of peak oil</a></div>
<p>To avoid the serious <a href="/wiki/Society" title="Society">social</a> and <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">economic</a> implications a global decline in oil production could entail, the 2005 Hirsch report emphasized the need to find alternatives, at least ten to twenty years before the peak, and to phase out the use of petroleum over that time. This was similar to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oil_phase-out_in_Sweden" title="Oil phase-out in Sweden">a plan proposed for Sweden</a> that same year. Such <a href="/wiki/Mitigation_of_peak_oil" title="Mitigation of peak oil">mitigation</a> could include energy conservation, fuel substitution, and the use of unconventional oil. Because mitigation can reduce the use of traditional petroleum sources, it can also affect the timing of peak oil and the shape of the <a href="/wiki/Hubbert_curve" title="Hubbert curve">Hubbert curve</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Energy_policy" title="Energy policy">Energy policy</a> may be reformed leading to greater <a href="/wiki/Energy_intensity" title="Energy intensity">energy intensity</a>, for example in <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_Gas_Rationing_Plan_in_Iran" title="2007 Gas Rationing Plan in Iran">2007 Gas Rationing Plan in Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Energy_Program" title="National Energy Program">National Energy Program</a> and in the USA with the <i><a href="/wiki/Energy_Independence_and_Security_Act_of_2007" title="Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007">Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007</a></i> also called the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clean_Energy_Act_of_2007" title="Clean Energy Act of 2007">Clean Energy Act of 2007</a></i>. Another mitigation measure is the setup of a cache of <a href="/wiki/Global_strategic_petroleum_reserves" title="Global strategic petroleum reserves">secure fuel reserves</a> like the United States <a href="/wiki/Strategic_Petroleum_Reserve_(United_States)" title="Strategic Petroleum Reserve (United States)">Strategic Petroleum Reserve</a>, in case of <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">national emergency</a>. <a href="/wiki/Energy_policy_of_China#Energy_conservation" title="Energy policy of China">Chinese energy policy</a> includes specific targets within their 5-year plans.</p>
<p><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_McKillop&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Andrew McKillop (page does not exist)">Andrew McKillop</a> has been a proponent of a contract and converge model or capping scheme, to mitigate both emissions of <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gases</a> and a peak oil crisis. The imposition of a <a href="/wiki/Carbon_tax" title="Carbon tax">carbon tax</a> would have mitigating effects on an oil crisis.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The Oil Depletion Protocol has been developed by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Heinberg" title="Richard Heinberg">Richard Heinberg</a> to implement a powerdown during a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hubbert_Peak_Theory" title="Hubbert Peak Theory">peak oil</a> crisis. While many <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a> and energy policy organisations have advocated reforms to <a href="/wiki/Energy_development" title="Energy development">energy development</a> from the 1970s, some cater to a specific crisis in energy supply including <a href="/wiki/Energy-Quest" title="Energy-Quest">Energy-Quest</a> and the <a href="/wiki/International_Association_for_Energy_Economics" title="International Association for Energy Economics">International Association for Energy Economics</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Oil_Depletion_Analysis_Centre" title="Oil Depletion Analysis Centre">Oil Depletion Analysis Centre</a> and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_the_Study_of_Peak_Oil_and_Gas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Association for the Study of Peak Oil and Gas (page does not exist)">Association for the Study of Peak Oil and Gas</a> examine the timing and likely effects of peak oil.</p>
<p>Ecologist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Rees_(academic)" title="William Rees (academic)">William Rees</a> believes that</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">

<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">To avoid a serious energy crisis in coming decades, citizens in the industrial countries should actually be urging their governments to come to international agreement on a persistent, orderly, predictable, and steepening series of oil and natural gas price hikes over the next two decades.</td>

<p>Due to a lack of political viability on the issue, government mandated fuel prices hikes are unlikely and the unresolved <a href="/wiki/Dilemma" title="Dilemma">dilemma</a> of fossil fuel dependence is becoming a <a href="/wiki/Wicked_problem" title="Wicked problem">wicked problem</a>. A global <a href="/wiki/Soft_energy_path" title="Soft energy path">soft energy path</a> seems improbable, due to the <a href="/wiki/Rebound_effect_(conservation)" title="Rebound effect (conservation)">rebound effect</a>. Conclusions that the world is heading towards an unprecedented large and potentially devastating global energy crisis due to a decline in the availability of cheap oil lead to calls for a decreasing dependency on <a href="/wiki/Fossil_fuel" title="Fossil fuel">fossil fuel</a>.</p>
<p>Other ideas concentrate on design and development of improved, energy-efficient urban infrastructure in developing nations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Government funding for alternative energy is more likely to increase during an energy crisis, so too are incentives for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oil_exploration" title="Oil exploration">oil exploration</a>. For example, funding for research into <a href="/wiki/Inertial_confinement_fusion" title="Inertial confinement fusion">inertial confinement fusion</a> technology increased during the 1970s.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kirk_Sorensen" title="Kirk Sorensen">Kirk Sorensen</a> and others<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Richard_Martin_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Richard_Martin-13">[13]</a></sup> have suggested that additional nuclear power plants, particularly <a href="/wiki/Liquid_fluoride_thorium_reactor" title="Liquid fluoride thorium reactor">liquid fluoride thorium reactors</a> have the <a href="/wiki/Energy_density" title="Energy density">energy density</a> to mitigate <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a> and replace the energy from <a href="/wiki/Peak_oil" title="Peak oil">peak oil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peak_coal" title="Peak coal">peak coal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peak_gas" title="Peak gas">peak gas</a>. The reactors produce electricity and heat so much of the transportation infrastructure should move over to electric vehicles. However, the high process heat of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Molten_salt_reactors" title="Molten salt reactors">molten salt reactors</a> could be used to make <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liquid_fuels" title="Liquid fuels">liquid fuels</a> from any carbon source.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s_oil_glut">2010s oil glut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_crisis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010s oil glut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2010s_oil_glut" title="2010s oil glut">2010s oil glut</a></div>
<p>Rather counterintuitively, the world economy has had to deal with the unforeseen consequences of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2016_oil_glut" title="2016 oil glut">2015-2016 oil glut</a> also known as <a href="/wiki/2010s_oil_glut" title="2010s oil glut">2010s oil glut</a>, a major energy crisis that took many experts by surprise. This <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oversupply" title="Oversupply">oversupply</a> crisis started with a considerable time-lag, more than six years after the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Recession" title="Great Recession">Great Recession</a>: "<i>the price of oil</i> [had] <i>stabilized at a relatively high level (around $100 a barrel) unlike all previous recessionary cycles since 1980 (start of First Persian Gulf War). But nothing guarantee[d] such price levels in perpetuity</i>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_and_economic_effects">Social and economic effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_crisis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Social and economic effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Energy_economics" title="Energy economics">Energy economics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy_commercialization" title="Renewable energy commercialization">Renewable energy commercialization</a></div>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Energy_and_the_Macroeconomy" title="Energy and the Macroeconomy">macroeconomic</a> implications of a <a href="/wiki/Supply_shock" title="Supply shock">supply shock</a>-induced energy crisis are large, because energy is the resource used to exploit all other resources. When <a href="/wiki/Energy_market" title="Energy market">energy markets</a> fail, an energy shortage develops. Electricity consumers may experience intentionally engineered <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rolling_blackout" title="Rolling blackout">rolling blackouts</a> during periods of insufficient supply or unexpected <a href="/wiki/Power_outage" title="Power outage">power outages</a>, regardless of the cause.</p>
<p>Industrialized nations are dependent on oil, and efforts to restrict the supply of oil would have an adverse effect on the economies of oil producers. For the consumer, the price of <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas_prices" title="Natural gas prices">natural gas</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Price_of_petroleum" title="Price of petroleum">gasoline</a> (petrol) and <a href="/wiki/Diesel_fuel" title="Diesel fuel">diesel</a> for cars and other vehicles rises. An early response from stakeholders is the call for reports, investigations and commissions into the price of fuels. There are also movements towards the development of more <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_urban_infrastructure" title="Sustainable urban infrastructure">sustainable urban infrastructure</a>.</p>

<p>In the market, new technology and <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">energy efficiency</a> measures become desirable for consumers seeking to decrease transport costs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> January 30, 2008 Planet Ark. Examples include:</p>
<ul>
<li>In 1980 <a href="/wiki/Briggs_%26_Stratton" title="Briggs &amp; Stratton">Briggs &amp; Stratton</a> developed the first gasoline <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hybrid_electric_automobile" title="Hybrid electric automobile">hybrid electric automobile</a>; also are appearing <a href="/wiki/Plug-in_hybrid" title="Plug-in hybrid">plug-in hybrids</a>.</li>
<li>the growth of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Advanced_biofuel" title="Advanced biofuel">advanced biofuels</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/Railway_electrification_system" title="Railway electrification system">Railway electrification systems</a> and new engines such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ganz-Mavag" title="Ganz-Mavag">Ganz-Mavag</a> locomotive</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Variable_compression_ratio" title="Variable compression ratio">variable compression ratio</a> for vehicles</li>
</ul>
<p>Other responses include the development of <a href="/wiki/Unconventional_oil" title="Unconventional oil">unconventional oil</a> sources such as <a href="/wiki/Synthetic_fuel" title="Synthetic fuel">synthetic fuel</a> from places like the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athabasca_Oil_Sands" title="Athabasca Oil Sands">Athabasca Oil Sands</a>, more <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy_commercialization" title="Renewable energy commercialization">renewable energy commercialization</a> and use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alternative_propulsion" title="Alternative propulsion">alternative propulsion</a>. There may be a <a href="/wiki/Localism_(politics)" title="Localism (politics)">Relocation</a> trend towards <a href="/wiki/Local_food" title="Local food">local foods</a> and possibly <a href="/wiki/Microgeneration" title="Microgeneration">microgeneration</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solar_thermal_collector" title="Solar thermal collector">solar thermal collectors</a> and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_energy" title="Green energy">green energy</a> sources.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">Tourism</a> trends and <a href="/wiki/Gas-guzzler" title="Gas-guzzler">gas-guzzler</a> ownership varies with fuel costs. Energy shortages can influence public opinion on subjects from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuclear_power_plants" title="Nuclear power plants">nuclear power plants</a> to electric blankets. Building <a href="/wiki/Construction" title="Construction">construction</a> techniques—improved <a href="/wiki/Building_insulation" title="Building insulation">insulation</a>, reflective roofs, thermally efficient windows, etc.—change to reduce heating costs.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Crisis_management">Crisis management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_crisis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Crisis management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An electricity shortage is felt most acutely in heating, <a href="/wiki/Cooking" title="Cooking">cooking</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Water_supply" title="Water supply">water supply</a>. Therefore, a sustained energy crisis may become a <a href="/wiki/Humanitarian_crisis" title="Humanitarian crisis">humanitarian crisis</a>.</p>
<p>If an energy shortage is prolonged a <a href="/wiki/Crisis_management" title="Crisis management">crisis management</a> phase is enforced by authorities. <a href="/wiki/Energy_audit" title="Energy audit">Energy audits</a> may be conducted to monitor usage. Various curfews with the intention of increasing <a href="/wiki/Energy_conservation" title="Energy conservation">energy conservation</a> may be initiated to reduce consumption. For example, to conserve power during the Central Asia energy crisis, authorities in Tajikistan ordered bars and cafes to operate by candlelight.<cite class="citation web"><a class="external text" href="http://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=18784716" rel="nofollow">"Crisis Looms as Bitter Cold, Blackouts Hit Tajikistan"</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/NPR" title="NPR">NPR</a></i><span class="reference-accessdate">. Retrieved <span class="nowrap">2008-02-10</span></span>.</cite><span class="Z3988" title="ctx_ver=Z39.88-2004&amp;rfr_id=info%3Asid%2Fen.wikipedia.org%3AEnergy+crisis&amp;rft.atitle=Crisis+Looms+as+Bitter+Cold%2C+Blackouts+Hit+Tajikistan&amp;rft.genre=unknown&amp;rft_id=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.npr.org%2Ftemplates%2Fstory%2Fstory.php%3FstoryId%3D18784716&amp;rft.jtitle=NPR&amp;rft_val_fmt=info%3Aofi%2Ffmt%3Akev%3Amtx%3Ajournal"><span style="display:none;"> </span></span></p>
<p>In the worst kind of energy crisis <a href="/wiki/Energy_rationing" title="Energy rationing">energy rationing</a> and fuel <a href="/wiki/Rationing" title="Rationing">rationing</a> may be incurred. <a href="/wiki/Panic_buying" title="Panic buying">Panic buying</a> may beset outlets as awareness of shortages spread. Facilities close down to save on heating oil; and factories cut production and lay off workers. The risk of <a href="/wiki/Stagflation" title="Stagflation">stagflation</a> increases.</p>
