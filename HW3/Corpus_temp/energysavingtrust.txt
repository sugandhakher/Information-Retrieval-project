<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><b>Energy Saving Trust</b> (EST) is a British organization devoted to promoting <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">energy efficiency</a>, <a href="/wiki/Energy_conservation" title="Energy conservation">energy conservation</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_energy" title="Sustainable energy">sustainable use of energy</a>, thereby reducing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide_emissions" title="Carbon dioxide emissions">carbon dioxide emissions</a> and helping to prevent man-made <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>. It was founded in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> as a government-sponsored initiative in 1992, following the global <a href="/wiki/Earth_Summit" title="Earth Summit">Earth Summit</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dawson_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-dawson-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Energy Saving Trust is an independent, not-for-profit organization funded by the government and the private sector.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-een_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-een-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enerdata_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-enerdata-3">[3]</a></sup> It is a <a href="/wiki/Social_enterprise" title="Social enterprise">social enterprise</a>, and also has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charitable_foundation" title="Charitable foundation">charitable foundation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-about-4">[4]</a></sup> The EST has regional offices in England, and country offices in Wales, Northern Ireland, and Scotland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dawson_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-dawson-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sust_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-sust-5">[5]</a></sup> It maintains a comprehensive website, and a network of numerous local advice centres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_purpose">History and purpose</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_Saving_Trust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and purpose">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Energy Saving Trust was formally established in November 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oshitani166_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-oshitani166-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enerdata_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-enerdata-3">[3]</a></sup> It was formed, as a public-private partnership, in response both to the director-general of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ofgas" title="Ofgas">Ofgas</a>'s 1991 proposal to increase energy efficiency in <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> use,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-owen1996_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-owen1996-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-owen1999_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-owen1999-10">[10]</a></sup> and to the global June 1992 <a href="/wiki/Earth_Summit" title="Earth Summit">Earth Summit</a> call to reduce <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> emissions and prevent global warming and climate change.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dawson_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-dawson-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-whitmarsh_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-whitmarsh-11">[11]</a></sup> In the wake of energy-supplier privatisation in the UK, the EST was also specifically formed as an instrument to ensure energy conservation and carbon-emission reduction in a free-market environment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oshitani186_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-oshitani186-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-owen1999_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-owen1999-10">[10]</a></sup> The structure, scope, nature, and funding of EST's activities and programmes have varied over the years due to governmental policy changes; however its primary focus – on consumers and households – has remained the same.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-whitmarsh_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-whitmarsh-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> It is the largest provider of energy-saving advice, and has effected significant and measurable savings of energy, money, and carbon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-whitmarsh_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-whitmarsh-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>EST's main goals are to achieve the sustainable use of energy and to cut carbon dioxide emissions. It acts as a bridge between consumers, government, trade, businesses, <a href="/wiki/Voluntary_sector" title="Voluntary sector">third sector</a> organisations, local authorities, and the energy market.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-een_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-een-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-about-4">[4]</a></sup> The EST's target audience is consumers, local authorities, energy companies, and policy makers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-manage_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-manage-14">[14]</a></sup> Among other activities, they provide:</p>
<ul>
<li>Free advice, information, and action plans to individuals, organizations, communities, consumers, and the private sector on how to reduce carbon emissions, use water more sustainably, and save money on energy bills<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sust_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-sust-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-een_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-een-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-about-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li>Grants and grant-finding advice for energy-saving projects, installations, and purchases<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sust_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-sust-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carrington_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-carrington-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li>Energy-saving certification, assurance, and accreditation services for businesses and consumer goods<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sust_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-sust-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>Independent and authoritative research, and policy analysis, in energy-conservation areas including household energy efficiency, low-carbon transport, renewable energy, and <a href="/wiki/Microgeneration" title="Microgeneration">microgeneration</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sust_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-sust-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-een_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-een-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>Management or delivery of government programmes<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-about-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>Testing of low-carbon technology<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-about-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>Development of energy-efficient models and tools<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-about-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services_provided">Services provided</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_Saving_Trust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Services provided">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The EST provides grants and free advice to the public to help reduce energy use, energy bills, and greenhouse gas emissions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carrington_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-carrington-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>For individuals, Energy Saving Trust provides information and advice on subjects including:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ccc_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-ccc-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Insulation</li>
<li>Heating and hot water</li>
<li>Electricity use, green electricity, and energy-efficient products and appliances</li>
<li>Generating renewable energy</li>
<li>Finding and starting community projects</li>
<li>Energy-saving travel and transport</li>
</ul>
<p>For organisations, Energy Saving Trust provides numerous services including:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ccc_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-ccc-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_Deal" title="Green Deal">Green Deal</a> and other certifications</li>
<li>Advice and analysis</li>
<li>Technology and technical resources</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Transport" title="Transport">Transport</a> checks, advice, information, and green certifications</li>
<li>An assortment of government and local programmes</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/International" title="International">International</a> action, advice, and <a href="/wiki/Bespoke" title="Bespoke">bespoke</a> consultations</li>
</ul>
