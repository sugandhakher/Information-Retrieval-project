<p><b>Peak demand</b> is used to refer to a historically high point in the sales record of a particular <a href="/wiki/Product_(business)" title="Product (business)">product</a>. In terms of energy use, peak demand describes a period of simultaneous, strong consumer <a href="/wiki/Supply_and_demand" title="Supply and demand">demand</a> or a period of highest demand in a billing period.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Peak_load">Peak load</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peak_demand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Peak load">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Peak demand, <b>peak load</b> or <b>on-peak</b> are terms used in <a href="/wiki/Energy_demand_management" title="Energy demand management">energy demand management</a> describing a period in which <a href="/wiki/Electric_power" title="Electric power">electrical power</a> is expected to be provided for a sustained period at a significantly higher than average supply level. Peak demand fluctuations may occur on daily, monthly, seasonal and yearly cycles. For an <a href="/wiki/Electric_utility" title="Electric utility">electric utility</a> company, the actual point of peak demand is a single half-hour or hourly period which represents the highest point of customer consumption of electricity. At this time there is a combination of office, domestic demand and at some times of the year, the fall of darkness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iee_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-iee-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some utilities will charge customers based on their individual peak demand. The highest demand during each month or even a single 15 to 30 minute period of highest use in the previous year may be used to calculate charges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Load_profile" title="Load profile">Load profile</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Off-peak">Off-peak</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peak_demand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Off-peak">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Peak demand is considered to be the opposite to off-peak hours when power demand is usually low. There are off-peak <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Time-of-use&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Time-of-use (page does not exist)">time-of-use</a> <a href="/wiki/Rate_(mathematics)" title="Rate (mathematics)">rates</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Response">Response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peak_demand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Demand_response" title="Demand response">Demand response</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tagesgang_engl.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="612" data-file-width="792" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Tagesgang_engl.png/220px-Tagesgang_engl.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Tagesgang_engl.png/330px-Tagesgang_engl.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Tagesgang_engl.png/440px-Tagesgang_engl.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tagesgang_engl.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Typical daily consumption of electrical power in Germany</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The maximum demand dictates the size of generators, transmission lines, transformers and circuit breakers for utilities even if that amount lasts just one hour per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eurs_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-eurs-3">[3]</a></sup> Natural gas fueled power generators must all have adequately sized pipelines. Power generation which is able to be rapidly ramped up for peak demand often uses more expensive fuels, is less efficient and has higher marginal carbon emissions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eurs_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-eurs-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Peak demand may exceed the maximum supply levels that the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrical_power_industry" title="Electrical power industry">electrical power industry</a> can <a href="/wiki/Electricity_generation" title="Electricity generation">generate</a>, resulting in <a href="/wiki/Power_outage" title="Power outage">power outages</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Load_shedding" title="Load shedding">load shedding</a>. This often occurs during <a href="/wiki/Heat_wave" title="Heat wave">heat waves</a> when use of air conditioners and powered fans raises the rate of <a href="/wiki/Energy_consumption" title="Energy consumption">energy consumption</a> significantly. During a shortage authorities may request the public to curtail their energy use and shift it to a non-peak period.</p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Energy_crisis" title="Energy crisis">Energy crisis</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Power_stations">Power stations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peak_demand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Power stations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Power_station" title="Power station">Power stations</a> providing power to <a href="/wiki/Electrical_grid" title="Electrical grid">electrical grids</a> for peak demand are called <a href="/wiki/Peaking_power_plant" title="Peaking power plant">peaking power plants</a> or 'peakers'. <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">Natural gas</a> fueled power stations can be fired up rapidly and are therefore often utilized at peak demand times. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pumped_storage" title="Pumped storage">Pumped storage</a> type dams such as <a href="/wiki/Carters_Dam" title="Carters Dam">Carters Dam</a> help to meet peak demand as well.</p>
<p>The chances that a <a href="/wiki/Wind_farm" title="Wind farm">wind farm</a> will be unable to meet peak demand are greater than for a fossil-fueled power station, due to the ability to store liquid fuels for use during peak demand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
