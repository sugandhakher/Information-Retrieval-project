<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p>The <b>United Nations Conference on the Human Environment</b> was held in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> from June 5–16 in 1972.</p>
<p>When the UN General Assembly decided to convene the 1972 Stockholm Conference, at the initiative of the Government of Sweden to host it, UN Secretary-General <a href="/wiki/U_Thant" title="U Thant">U Thant</a> invited <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Strong" title="Maurice Strong">Maurice Strong</a> to lead it as Secretary-General of the Conference, as the Canadian diplomat (under <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Trudeau" title="Pierre Trudeau">Pierre Trudeau</a>) had initiated and already worked for over two years on the project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Strong_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Strong-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Conference_on_the_Human_Environment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sweden first suggested to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ECOSOC" title="ECOSOC">ECOSOC</a> in 1968 the idea of having a UN conference to focus on human interactions with the environment. ECOSOC passed resolution 1346 supporting the idea. General Assembly Resolution 2398 in 1969 decided to convene a conference in 1972 and mandated a set of reports from the UN secretary-general suggesting that the conference focus on "stimulating and providing guidelines for action by national government and international organizations" facing environmental issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DeSombre_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-DeSombre-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stockholm_Declaration">Stockholm Declaration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Conference_on_the_Human_Environment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stockholm Declaration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The meeting agreed upon a <a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_the_United_Nations_Conference_on_the_Human_Environment" title="Declaration of the United Nations Conference on the Human Environment">Declaration containing 26 principles concerning the environment and development</a>; an Action Plan with 109 recommendations, and a Resolution.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baylis3_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Baylis3-4">[4]</a></sup> Principles of the Stockholm Declaration:<br/>
1. Human rights must be asserted, apartheid and <a href="/wiki/Colonialism" title="Colonialism">colonialism</a> condemned<br/>
2. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natural_resources" title="Natural resources">Natural resources</a> must be safeguarded<br/>
3. The Earth's capacity to produce <a href="/wiki/Renewable_resource" title="Renewable resource">renewable resources</a> must be maintained<br/>
4. Wildlife must be safeguarded<br/>
5. Non-renewable resources must be shared and not exhausted<br/>
6. Pollution must not exceed the environment's capacity to clean itself<br/>
7. Damaging oceanic pollution must be prevented<br/>
8. Development is needed to improve the environment<br/>
9. Developing countries therefore need assistance<br/>
10. Developing countries need reasonable prices for exports to carry out environmental management<br/>
11. Environment policy must not hamper development<br/>
12. Developing countries need money to develop environmental safeguards<br/>
13. Integrated development planning is needed<br/>
14. Rational planning should resolve conflicts between environment and development<br/>
15. Human settlements must be planned to eliminate environmental problems<br/>
16. Governments should plan their own appropriate population policies<br/>
17. National institutions must plan development of states' natural resources<br/>
18. Science and technology must be used to improve the environment<br/>
19. <a href="/wiki/Environmental_education" title="Environmental education">Environmental education</a> is essential<br/>
20. Environmental research must be promoted, particularly in developing countries<br/>
21. States may exploit their resources as they wish but must not endanger others<br/>
22. Compensation is due to states thus endangered<br/>
23. Each nation must establish its own standards<br/>
24. There must be cooperation on international issues<br/>
25. <a href="/wiki/International_organization" title="International organization">International organizations</a> should help to improve the environment<br/>
26. <a href="/wiki/Weapon_of_mass_destruction" title="Weapon of mass destruction">Weapons of mass destruction</a> must be eliminated<br/>
<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>One of the seminal issue that emerged from the conference is the recognition for poverty alleviation for protecting the environment. The Indian Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Indira_Gandhi" title="Indira Gandhi">Indira Gandhi</a> in her seminal speech in the conference brought forward the connection between ecological management and <a href="/wiki/Poverty_reduction" title="Poverty reduction">poverty alleviation</a>.</p>
<p>Some argue<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> that this conference, and more importantly the scientific conferences preceding it, had a real impact on the environmental policies of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Community" title="European Community">European Community</a> (that later became the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>). For example, in 1973, the EU created the Environmental and Consumer Protection Directorate, and composed the first Environmental Action Program. Such increased interest and research collaboration arguably paved the way for further understanding of global warming, which has led to such agreements as the <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Protocol" title="Kyoto Protocol">Kyoto Protocol</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Paris_Agreement" title="Paris Agreement">Paris Agreement</a>, and has given a foundation of modern environmentalism.</p>
