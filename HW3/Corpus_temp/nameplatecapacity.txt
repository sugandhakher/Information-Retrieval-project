<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><b>Nameplate capacity</b>, also known as the <b>rated capacity</b>, <b>nominal capacity</b>, <b>installed capacity</b>, or <b>maximum effect</b>, is the intended full-load sustained output of a facility such as a <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Power_plant_(disambiguation)" title="Power plant (disambiguation)">power plant</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nceia_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nceia-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncnrc_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncnrc-2">[2]</a></sup> a <a href="/wiki/Chemical_plant" title="Chemical plant">chemical plant</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncchem_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncchem-3">[3]</a></sup> fuel plant,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nccan_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-nccan-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncrefine_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncrefine-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncethanol_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncethanol-6">[6]</a></sup> metal refinery,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncalcoa_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncalcoa-7">[7]</a></sup> mine,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncmine_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncmine-8">[8]</a></sup> and many others. Nameplate capacity is the number registered with authorities for classifying the <a href="/wiki/Power_(physics)" title="Power (physics)">power</a> output of a power station usually expressed in <a href="/wiki/Watt#Megawatt" title="Watt">megawatts (MW)</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdod_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdod-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Power plants with an output consistently near their nameplate capacity have a high <a href="/wiki/Capacity_factor" title="Capacity factor">capacity factor</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dispatchable_power">Dispatchable power</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nameplate_capacity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Dispatchable power">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/Dispatchable_generation" title="Dispatchable generation">dispatchable power</a>, this capacity depends on the internal technical capability of the plant to maintain output for a reasonable amount of time (for example, a day), neither momentarily nor permanently, and without considering external events such as lack of fuel or internal events such as maintenance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncbelow_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncbelow-10">[10]</a></sup> Actual output can be different from nameplate capacity for a number of reasons depending on equipment and circumstances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncbelow_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-ncbelow-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncabove_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncabove-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-dispatchable_power">Non-dispatchable power</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nameplate_capacity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Non-dispatchable power">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/Intermittent_energy_source" title="Intermittent energy source">non-dispatchable power</a>, particularly <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a>, nameplate capacity refers to generation under ideal conditions. Output is generally limited by weather conditions, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydroelectric_dam" title="Hydroelectric dam">hydroelectric dam</a> water levels, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tidal" title="Tidal">tidal</a> variations and other outside forces. Equipment failures and maintenance usually contribute less to capacity factor reduction than the innate variation of the power source. In <a href="/wiki/Photovoltaics" title="Photovoltaics">photovoltaics</a>, capacity is rated under <i>Standard Test Conditions</i> usually expressed in the non-<a href="/wiki/International_System_of_Units" title="International System of Units">SI</a> compliant unit <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Watt-peak" title="Watt-peak">watt-peak</a> (W<sub>p</sub>). In addition, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PV_system" title="PV system">PV system</a>'s nameplate capacity is sometimes denoted by a subindex, for example, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Watt-peak_AC" title="Watt-peak AC">MW<sub>DC</sub> or MW<sub>AC</sub></a>, in order to clarify, whether the raw <a href="/wiki/Direct_current" title="Direct current">DC</a> or already converted <a href="/wiki/Alternating_current" title="Alternating current">AC</a> power output is referred to.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Generator_capacity">Generator capacity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nameplate_capacity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Generator capacity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

Diagram of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turbo-electric" title="Turbo-electric">turbo-electric</a> <a href="/wiki/Combined_gas_and_steam" title="Combined gas and steam">COGES</a> power-plant, with power-consuming pump</div>

<p>The term is connected with <a href="/wiki/Nameplate" title="Nameplate">nameplates</a> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrical_generator" title="Electrical generator">electrical generators</a> as these plates describing the model name and manufacturer usually also contain the rated output,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nc_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-nc-12">[12]</a></sup> but the rated output of a power station to the <a href="/wiki/Electrical_grid" title="Electrical grid">electrical grid</a> is invariably less than the generator nameplate capacity, because the components connecting the actual generator to the "grid" also use power. Thus there is a distinction between component capacity and facility capacity.</p>
