<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><b>Energy efficiency</b> may refer to:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Energy_efficiency_(physics)" title="Energy efficiency (physics)">Energy efficiency (physics)</a>, the ratio between the useful output and input of an energy conversion process
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Electrical_efficiency" title="Electrical efficiency">Electrical efficiency</a>, useful power output per electrical power consumed</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mechanical_efficiency" title="Mechanical efficiency">Mechanical efficiency</a>, a ratio of the measured performance to the performance of an ideal machine</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thermal_efficiency" title="Thermal efficiency">Thermal efficiency</a>, the extent to which the energy added by heat is converted to net work output or vice versa</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luminous_efficiency" title="Luminous efficiency">Luminous efficiency</a>, a measure of how well a light source produces visible light</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fuel_efficiency" title="Fuel efficiency">Fuel efficiency</a>, the efficiency of converting potential energy in a fuel into kinetic energy</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Energy_efficiency_in_transportation" title="Energy efficiency in transportation">Energy efficiency in transportation</a>, the fuel economy of various modes of transportation</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">Efficient energy use</a>, minimizing the amount of energy used for a given, constant energy service</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Energy_conservation" title="Energy conservation">Energy conservation</a>, reducing energy consumption by using less of an energy service</li>
</ul>
