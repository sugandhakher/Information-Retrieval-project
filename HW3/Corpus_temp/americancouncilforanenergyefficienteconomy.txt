<p>The <b>American Council for an Energy-Efficient Economy</b>, or ACEEE, is a <a href="/wiki/Nonprofit_organization" title="Nonprofit organization">nonprofit</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/501(c)#501.28c.29.283.29" title="501(c)">501(c)(3)</a> organization. Founded in 1980, ACEEE's mission is to act as a catalyst to advance energy efficiency policies, programs, technologies, investments, and behaviors in order to help achieve greater economic prosperity, energy security, and environmental protection.</p>
<p>ACEEE promotes energy efficiency by conducting technical and policy analyses; advising policymakers and program managers; and working collaboratively with businesses, government officials, public interest groups, and other organizations. It convenes conferences and workshops, primarily for energy efficiency professionals, and produces reports, books, conference proceedings, and media outreach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>ACEEE employs more than 35 <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>-based employees, and holds field offices in Delaware, Michigan, Washington, and Wisconsin. The organization's primary focuses are on end-use efficiency in <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industry</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buildings" title="Buildings">buildings</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Utilities" title="Utilities">utilities</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transportation" title="Transportation">transportation</a>; <a href="/wiki/Economic_impact_analysis" title="Economic impact analysis">economic analysis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Human_behavior" title="Human behavior">human behavior</a>; and state and national <a href="/wiki/Public_policy" title="Public policy">policy</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Federal_and_State_Energy_Policy">Federal and State Energy Policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Council_for_an_Energy-Efficient_Economy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Federal and State Energy Policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Federal_Policy">Federal Policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Council_for_an_Energy-Efficient_Economy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Federal Policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>ACEEE has worked on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_energy_policy" title="US energy policy">federal energy policy</a> since the 1980s. The organization played central roles in the development of the <a href="/wiki/National_Appliance_Energy_Conservation_Act" title="National Appliance Energy Conservation Act">National Appliance Energy Conservation Act</a> of 1987, energy efficiency provisions in the <a href="/wiki/Energy_Policy_Act_of_1992" title="Energy Policy Act of 1992">Energy Policy Act of 1992</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Energy_Policy_Act_of_2005" title="Energy Policy Act of 2005">Energy Policy Act of 2005</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Energy_Independence_and_Security_Act_of_2007" title="Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007">Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007</a>. It also played a role in the development of energy efficiency sections in recent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._farm_bill" title="U.S. farm bill">farm bills</a>. Many of these provisions were developed in cooperation with interested business and received <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bipartisan" title="Bipartisan">bipartisan</a> support.</p>
<p>ACEEE staff testify before <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">Congress</a> and work closely with Congressional staff in both <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">parties</a> to help shape new initiatives and analyze the impacts of energy and climate policy proposals. They weigh in on the <a href="/wiki/United_States_budget_process" title="United States budget process">federal budget process</a>, promoting and increasing funding for what they deem the most effective energy efficiency programs.</p>
<p>The organization also works with federal agencies such as the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">Environmental Protection Agency</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Energy" title="United States Department of Energy">Department of Energy</a> on programs and policies, and participates in formal rulemakings on energy efficiency issues. Most of ACEEE's federal policy work involves research and education; however, they do a limited amount of <a href="/wiki/Lobbying" title="Lobbying">lobbying</a> using unrestricted funds.</p>
<p>They supported the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EPS_Service_Parts_Act_of_2014_(H.R._5057;_113th_Congress)" title="EPS Service Parts Act of 2014 (H.R. 5057; 113th Congress)">EPS Service Parts Act of 2014 (H.R. 5057; 113th Congress)</a>, a bill that would exempt certain <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/External_power_supply" title="External power supply">external power supplies</a> from complying with standards set forth in a final rule published by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Energy" title="United States Department of Energy">United States Department of Energy</a> in February 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbo5057_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-cbo5057-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ITIhankin_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ITIhankin-3">[3]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_Committee_on_Energy_and_Commerce" title="United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce">United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce</a> describes the bill as a bill that "provides regulatory relief by making a simple technical correction to the <a href="/wiki/Energy_Independence_and_Security_Act_of_2007" title="Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007">2007 Energy Independence and Security Act</a> to exempt certain power supply (EPS) service and spare parts from federal efficiency standards."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ECCpr_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ECCpr-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="State_Policy">State Policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Council_for_an_Energy-Efficient_Economy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: State Policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the state level, ACEEE works closely with public officials and local energy efficiency advocates, providing advice, analysis, and technical support. In order to maximize its impact, the organization concentrates its efforts on large states that are poised to make major decisions on energy efficiency policy issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> For example, in 2007 and 2008, it emphasized work in <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>. All of these states passed significant energy efficiency legislation, with additional legislation and regulations likely in some of them.</p>
<p>ACEEE also works on state policies that can potentially be adopted at the national level. For example, staff have worked with several states to develop state appliance and equipment <a href="/wiki/Minimum_energy_performance_standard" title="Minimum energy performance standard">efficiency standards</a>, which have subsequently been adopted by Congress.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research_and_Analysis">Research and Analysis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Council_for_an_Energy-Efficient_Economy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Research and Analysis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ACEEE devotes much of its resources to research and analysis in order to discern the best technical, program, and policy practices that promote efficient energy use, and to define the magnitude of benefits of energy efficiency. Research staff survey market trends and activities, analyze technical and economic potential for energy efficiency, seek to understand consumer energy decisions, and assess the potential for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regulations" title="Regulations">regulations</a>, policies, and programs to achieve energy savings. ACEEE's research programs include:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Buildings and equipment</b> - ACEEE's oldest research program analyzes opportunities for energy efficiency in <a href="/wiki/Home_appliance" title="Home appliance">appliances</a> and equipment; <a href="/wiki/Green_building" title="Green building">building design</a>, <a href="/wiki/Construction" title="Construction">construction</a>, and operation; and programs and policies that encourage consumers to make energy-efficient product choices.</li>
<li><b>Utilities</b> - Serves as the principal documenter and reviewer of publicly funded energy efficiency programs and supporting policies, preparing regular surveys of best practices and supporting implementers and <a href="/wiki/Regulatory_agency" title="Regulatory agency">regulators</a> in designing the next generation of programs and policies.</li>
<li><b>Transportation</b> - Tracks vehicle technology and estimates the potential for future energy efficiency. In addition, the program explores technologies and policies that encourage changes in personal and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freight" title="Freight">freight</a> transportation markets to further reduce energy use.</li>
<li><b>Industry</b> - Applies unique experience in energy efficiency for the <a href="/wiki/Manufacturing" title="Manufacturing">manufacturing</a> sector (including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Distributed_Energy_Resources" title="Distributed Energy Resources">distributed energy resources</a> such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Combined_heat_and_power" title="Combined heat and power">combined heat and power</a>), and assesses <a href="/wiki/Emerging_technologies" title="Emerging technologies">technologies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Best_practice" title="Best practice">practices</a>, programs, and policies to achieve increased energy efficiency in manufacturing.</li>
<li><b>Agriculture</b> - Addresses the unique energy efficiency program and policy needs of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Farms" title="Farms">farms</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rural_communities" title="Rural communities">rural communities</a> through assessment of energy use patterns, and opportunities for more energy efficiency practices and technologies.</li>
<li><b>Economic analysis</b> - Encourages the use of sound <a href="/wiki/Economic_model" title="Economic model">economic modeling</a> practices, studies the impacts of energy policies on the economy, and projects the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cost-benefit_analysis" title="Cost-benefit analysis">economic benefits</a> of energy efficiency technologies, practices, and policies.</li>
<li><b>State analysis</b> - Cross-program effort that combines expertise from other research programs to assess state energy policies, analyze the potential for energy efficiency at the state level, and collect key resources to support state policymakers, program implementers, and efficiency advocates.</li>
<li><b>International</b> - Works with colleagues from transitional economies to adapt technology, program, and policy experiences from the United States to these economies.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Outreach">Outreach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Council_for_an_Energy-Efficient_Economy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Outreach">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ACEEE attempts to reach out to and inform diverse audiences in a variety of ways:</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Conferences">Conferences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Council_for_an_Energy-Efficient_Economy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Conferences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>ACEEE holds several conferences each year that aim to bring together disparate stakeholders to focus on multiple aspects of energy efficiency and the role it plays in addressing critical issues such as <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Energy_resources" title="Energy resources">energy resources</a>, utility structure and regulation, and energy use in buildings, industry, and agriculture. Recent conferences include:</p>
<ul>
<li>2013 Summer Study on Energy Efficiency in Industry</li>
<li>2012 Summer Study on Energy Efficiency in Buildings</li>
<li>Hot Water Forum: Water Heating, Distribution, and Use Efficiency</li>
<li>Energy Efficiency Finance Forum</li>
<li>National Symposium on Market Transformation</li>
<li>Forum on Energy Efficiency and Agriculture</li>
<li>Behavior, Energy, and Climate Change Conference (BECC)</li>
<li>National Conference on Energy Efficiency as a Resource</li>
</ul>

<p>ACEEE provides research and technical analysis of current energy efficiency policies and practices, as well as forecasting future trends.
