<p>The <b>World Sustainable Energy Days</b> is an annual <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a> conference held in <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>. It hosts events centred on sustainable energy production and use, which covers energy efficiency and <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a> sources for buildings, industry and transport. The events that take place during the World Sustainable Energy Days present the latest technology trends, outstanding examples and European strategies and offer opportunities to establish new partnerships. The conference raises awareness of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_energy" title="Green energy">green energy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">energy efficiency</a>.</p>
<p>Since 1992 every year in March experts and decision makers from all over the world flock to <a href="/wiki/Upper_Austria" title="Upper Austria">Upper Austria</a> to attend the events - in general the conference attracts between 900 and 1,000 participants from 55-60 countries.</p>
<p>In parallel to the World Sustainable Energy Days, the <a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energiesparmesse_Wels" title="de:Energiesparmesse Wels">Energiesparmesse</a>, an exhibition and trade show dedicated to renewable energy sources and energy efficiency, is held. It attracts around 100,000 visitors and around 1,000 exhibiting companies.</p>
<p>The World Sustainable Energy Days are organised by the O.Ö. Energiesparverband (<a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/O.%C3%96._Energiesparverband" title="de:O.Ö. Energiesparverband">O.Ö. Energiesparverband</a> in German), the regional energy agency of Upper Austria.</p>
