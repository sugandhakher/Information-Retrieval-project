<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p>A <b>MEPS (Minimum Energy Performance Standard)</b> is a specification, containing a number of performance requirements for an <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a>-using device, that effectively limits the maximum amount of energy that may be consumed by a product in performing a specified task.</p>
<p>A MEPS is usually made mandatory by a government energy efficiency body. It may include requirements not directly related to energy; this is to ensure that general performance and user satisfaction are not adversely affected by increasing energy efficiency.</p>
<p>A MEPS generally requires use of a particular test procedure that specifies how performance is measured.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> when addressing energy efficiency, a MEPS is sometimes referred to simply as a “standard”, as in “Co-operation on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collaborative_Labeling_and_Appliance_Standards_Program_(CLASP)" title="Collaborative Labeling and Appliance Standards Program (CLASP)">Labeling and Standards Programs</a>”. In <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a> when addressing energy efficiency, MEPS are sometimes referred to as <i>Normas</i> (translated as "norms").</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minimum_energy_performance_standard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Refrigerator" title="Refrigerator">refrigerating appliance</a> is required to maintain <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperatures</a> inside its compartments within specified limits, and to operate (including defrosting) in a specified ambient temperature while using at most a specified amount of <a href="/wiki/Electricity" title="Electricity">electricity</a>; the energy use allowed varies according to volume, number of doors, the function of the various compartments and other parameters. This graph shows the dramatic reduction in electricity use in U.S. refrigerators following the introduction of a series of first California then U.S. MEPS starting in the mid-1970s:</li>
</ul>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kwh_per_frig.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="505" data-file-width="715" height="155" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Kwh_per_frig.jpg/220px-Kwh_per_frig.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Kwh_per_frig.jpg/330px-Kwh_per_frig.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Kwh_per_frig.jpg/440px-Kwh_per_frig.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

Price, volume and efficiency of refrigerators</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li>An <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electric_fan" title="Electric fan">electric fan</a> is required to shift air at a specified rate while consuming a limited amount of power.</li>
</ul>
<p>A storage <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_heater" title="Water heater">water heater</a> providing hot water for sanitary purposes is required to heat up a specified quantity of water to a specified temperature and store it at that temperature for a specified time while consuming a limited amount of energy. In this example, the requirements for heating up and for maintaining the temperature may be applied as two separate energy performance requirements or there may be a single task efficiency.</p>
<ul>
<li>An electric <a href="/wiki/Induction_motor" title="Induction motor">induction motor</a> is required to have a specified minimum full-load efficiency.</li>
</ul>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Compact_fluorescent_lamp" title="Compact fluorescent lamp">compact fluorescent lamp</a> is required to start and run up to near full <a href="/wiki/Brightness" title="Brightness">brightness</a> in a given time, to have a minimum life of several thousand hours, to maintain its output within specified limits, to withstand a certain number of switchings, to have a consistent colour appearance and a specified colour rendering. Its energy performance requirement is usually stated in terms of minimum efficacy (light output per electrical input).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="California">California</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minimum_energy_performance_standard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: California">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the U.S., the state of <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> was a pioneer <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> in the introduction of MEPS. In order to reduce the growth in electricity use, the <a href="/wiki/California_Energy_Commission" title="California Energy Commission">California Energy Commission</a> (CEC) was given unique and strong authority to regulate the efficiency of appliances sold in the state. It started to adopt appliance efficiency regulations in 1978, and has updated the standards regularly over time, and expanded the list of covered appliances.</p>
<p>In 1988, California's standards became national standards for the U.S. through the enactment of the <a href="/wiki/National_Appliance_Energy_Conservation_Act" title="National Appliance Energy Conservation Act">National Appliance Energy Conservation Act</a> (NAECA). The federal standards preempted state standards (unless the state justified a waiver from federal preemption based on conditions in the state), and since then, the U.S. Department of Energy has had the responsibility to update the federal standards.</p>
<p>California has continued to expand the list of appliances it regulates for appliances that are not federally regulated, and therefore not preempted. In recent years,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (April 2014)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> the CEC's attention has been focused on consumer electronics, for which energy use has been growing dramatically.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Brazil">Brazil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minimum_energy_performance_standard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Brazil">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A law was approved in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> MEPS have been set for three-phase electric motors and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compact_fluorescent_lamps" title="Compact fluorescent lamps">compact fluorescent lamps</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Zealand">New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minimum_energy_performance_standard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 5 February 2002, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> introduced Minimum Energy Performance Standards (MEPS) with Energy Efficiency Regulations. MEPS and energy rating labels help improve the energy efficiency of our<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (April 2014)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> products, and enable consumers to choose products that use less energy. Products covered by MEPS must meet or exceed set levels for energy performance before they can be sold to consumers. MEPS have been updated over the years (2002, 2003, 2004, 2008, 2011) to cover a wide range of products, and increasing levels of stringency. <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> works with Australia to harmonise MEPS levels. Almost all of its standards are joint standards with Australia. New Zealand has mandatory Energy rating labelling for dishwashers and clothes dryers, fridges, washing machines and room air conditioners. MEPS apply to the following:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Refrigerators and freezers</li>
<li>Washing machines</li>
<li>Air conditioners</li>
<li>Computer room air conditioners</li>
<li>Chillers</li>
<li>Electric water storage heaters</li>
<li>Gas water heaters</li>
<li>External power supplies</li>
<li>Set-top boxes</li>
<li>Distribution transformers</li>
<li>Refrigerated display cabinets</li>
<li>Three-phase electric motors</li>
<li>Ballasts for fluorescent lamps</li>
<li>Tubular fluorescent lamps</li>
</ul>
