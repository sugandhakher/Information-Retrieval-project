<p>The <b>Alliance to Save Energy</b> is a coalition consisting largely of industrial, technological, and energy corporations. The Alliance states that its mission is to "support <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">energy efficiency</a> as a cost-effective energy resource under existing market conditions and advocate energy-efficiency policies that minimize costs to society and individual consumers, and that lessen <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> emissions and their impact on the global climate." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The alliance's chief activities include <a href="/wiki/Public_relations" title="Public relations">public relations</a>, research, and <a href="/wiki/Lobbying" title="Lobbying">lobbying</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> to change <a href="/wiki/Energy_policy_of_the_United_States" title="Energy policy of the United States">U.S. energy policy</a>.</p>
<p>The creation of the Alliance was announced on February 10, 1977 with the support of the then U.S. President <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Carter" title="Jimmy Carter">Jimmy Carter</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It was the initiative of senators <a href="/wiki/Charles_H._Percy" title="Charles H. Percy">Charles Percy</a> and Hubert Humphrey.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Member_organizations">Member organizations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alliance_to_Save_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Member organizations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The alliance includes over 170 organizations committed to energy efficiency as a primary way to achieve the nation's environmental, economic, energy security and affordable housing goals.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Support_of_Energy_Independence_and_Security_Act_of_2007">Support of Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alliance_to_Save_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Support of Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2007, Alliance to Save Energy came out in support of the <a href="/wiki/Energy_Independence_and_Security_Act_of_2007" title="Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007">Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> which <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Banning_of_incandescent_lightbulbs" title="Banning of incandescent lightbulbs">phases out</a> the use of inefficient forms of <a href="/wiki/Incandescent_light_bulb" title="Incandescent light bulb">incandescent light bulbs</a> in favor of more energy-efficient lights, such as CFL, halogen incandescents, and LED lights. The purchase price of more efficient lights is typically higher than the purchase price of legacy incandescents. However, the low energy consumption and long life span of high-efficiency lights result in lower <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Life_cycle_assessment" title="Life cycle assessment">life-cycle costs</a> than their legacy incandescent counterparts. Several of the founding member corporations (including <a href="/wiki/Philips" title="Philips">Philips</a>, <a href="/wiki/Panasonic" title="Panasonic">Panasonic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Osram_Sylvania" title="Osram Sylvania">Sylvania</a>) are manufacturers of high-efficiency bulbs. However, all of these corporations are also manufacturers of the incandescent light bulbs that will be phased out of production following passage of the Clean Energy Bill of 2007.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="CarbonCount">CarbonCount</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alliance_to_Save_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: CarbonCount">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>CarbonCount is a metric developed by the Alliance to Save Energy that quantifies the impact of investments in U.S.-based energy-efficiency and renewable-energy projects given the expected reduction in carbon dioxide (CO<sub>2</sub>) emissions resulting from each $1,000 of investment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2015, Bloomberg New Energy Finance honored CarbonCount with its Finance for Resilience (FiRE) award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> FiRE is an open and action-oriented platform that collects, develops and helps implement powerful ideas to accelerate finance for clean energy, climate, sustainability and green growth. FiRe singles out ideas that have the potential for incremental finance of at least $1bn in clean energy in the first three years of implementation, that are achievable within 1-3 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Hannon Armstrong's 2015 issuance of Sustainable Yield Bonds secured by a portion of its utility scale solar and wind real estate related assets was the first investment to be certified under the CarbonCount methodology, receiving a CarbonCount score of 0.39 metric tons of CO<sub>2</sub> offset per $1000 of investment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In 2016, Deutsche Bank received a CarbonCount score of 0.18 metric tons of CO<sub>2</sub> offset per $1000 of investment in a portfolio of rooftop solar PV systems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legislation">Legislation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alliance_to_Save_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Legislation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Alliance to Save Energy supported the bill <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/To_require_the_Secretary_of_Energy_to_prepare_a_report_on_the_impact_of_thermal_insulation_on_both_energy_and_water_use_for_potable_hot_water_(H.R._4801;_113th_Congress)" title="To require the Secretary of Energy to prepare a report on the impact of thermal insulation on both energy and water use for potable hot water (H.R. 4801; 113th Congress)">To require the Secretary of Energy to prepare a report on the impact of thermal insulation on both energy and water use for potable hot water (H.R. 4801; 113th Congress)</a>, which would require the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_Energy" title="United States Secretary of Energy">United States Secretary of Energy</a> to prepare a report on the effects that <a href="/wiki/Thermal_insulation" title="Thermal insulation">thermal insulation</a> has on both energy consumption and systems for providing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Potable_water" title="Potable water">potable water</a> in <a href="/wiki/Category:Buildings_of_the_United_States_government" title="Category:Buildings of the United States government">federal buildings</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbo4801_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-cbo4801-13">[13]</a></sup> They called the bill a step "in the right direction" and saying that they "are hopeful energy efficiency can once again be an area of common ground between parties."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ASEcommitteepass_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-ASEcommitteepass-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>They supported the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EPS_Service_Parts_Act_of_2014_(H.R._5057;_113th_Congress)" title="EPS Service Parts Act of 2014 (H.R. 5057; 113th Congress)">EPS Service Parts Act of 2014 (H.R. 5057; 113th Congress)</a>, a bill that would exempt certain <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/External_power_supply" title="External power supply">external power supplies</a> from complying with standards set forth in a final rule published by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Energy" title="United States Department of Energy">United States Department of Energy</a> in February 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbo5057_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-cbo5057-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ITIhankin_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-ITIhankin-16">[16]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_Committee_on_Energy_and_Commerce" title="United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce">United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce</a> describes the bill as a bill that "provides regulatory relief by making a simple technical correction to the <a href="/wiki/Energy_Independence_and_Security_Act_of_2007" title="Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007">2007 Energy Independence and Security Act</a> to exempt certain power supply (EPS) service and spare parts from federal efficiency standards."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ECCpr_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-ECCpr-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Board_of_directors">Board of directors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alliance_to_Save_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Board of directors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The board includes CEOs, presidents and senior executives of companies, associations, consumer and environmental organizations, as well as officials from state government, universities and law firms.</p>
<p>The first board of directors and board of advisors were chaired by Senator Percy and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henry_A._Kissinger" title="Henry A. Kissinger">Henry A. Kissinger</a>, respectively. Honorary chairmen included Senators <a href="/wiki/Daniel_J._Evans" title="Daniel J. Evans">Daniel J. Evans</a>, <a href="/wiki/H._John_Heinz_III" title="H. John Heinz III">H. John Heinz III</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Timothy_E._Wirth" title="Timothy E. Wirth">Timothy E. Wirth</a>.</p>

(June 2013)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> board is chaired by Senator <a href="/wiki/Mark_Warner" title="Mark Warner">Mark Warner</a> (D-VA) and co-chaired by Tom King, President of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Grid_USA" title="National Grid USA">National Grid USA</a>.
